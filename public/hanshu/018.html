自古受命及中興之君，必興滅繼絕，修廢舉逸，然後天下歸仁，四方之政行焉。 傳稱武王克殷，追存賢聖，至乎不及下車。 世代雖殊，其揆一也。 高帝撥亂誅暴，庶事草創，日不暇給，然猶修祀六國，求聘四皓，過魏則寵無忌之墓，適趙則封樂毅之後。 及其行賞而授位也，爵以功為先後，官用能為次序。 後嗣共己遵業，舊臣繼踵居位。 至乎孝武，元功宿將略盡。 會上亦興文學，進拔幽隱，公孫弘自海瀕而登宰相，於是寵以列侯之爵。 又疇咨前代，詢問耆老，初得周後，復加爵邑。 自是之後，宰相畢侯矣。 元、成之間，晚得殷世，以備賓位。
Since antiquity, every ruler who received the Mandate and every sovereign who restored a dynasty invariably revived the fallen, continued the severed, restored the neglected, and elevated the overlooked. Only then did the realm embrace benevolence and governance extend to every quarter. Tradition holds that when King Wu conquered the Yin, he sought out and honored the worthy and sage with such urgency that he did not even pause to dismount from his chariot. Though the ages differ, the guiding principle remains the same. Emperor Gao set the disordered age aright and punished the violent. Though all affairs were still in their infancy and the days never long enough, he restored the sacrifices of the Six States, sought out the Four White-Haired Elders, honored Lord Wuji's tomb when passing through Wei, and enfeoffed Yue Yi's descendants when visiting Zhao. In dispensing rewards and bestowing positions, noble rank followed the order of merit, and office followed the order of ability. His successors reverently upheld his legacy, and the old ministers followed one upon another in their posts. By the reign of Emperor Wu, the founding officials and veteran generals had nearly all passed away. The emperor also promoted literary learning and elevated those dwelling in obscurity. Gongsun Hong rose from the shores of the sea to become chancellor and was honored with the rank of a ranked marquis. He also consulted the records of former ages and questioned the elders. Having found descendants of the Zhou, he bestowed upon them noble rank and fiefs. From this time onward, every chancellor was made a marquis. Between the reigns of Emperors Yuan and Cheng, descendants of the Yin were at last located and installed in the guest position.

漢興，外戚與定天下，侯者二人。 故誓曰：「非劉氏不王，若有亡功非上所置而侯者，天下共誅之。」 是以高后欲王諸呂，王陵廷爭； 孝景將侯王氏，脩侯犯色。 卒用廢黜。 是後薄昭、竇嬰、上官、衛、霍之侯，以功受爵。 其餘后父據□秋褒紀之義，帝舅緣大雅申伯之意，寖廣博矣。 是以別而敘之。
When the Han dynasty arose, two imperial relatives by marriage helped pacify the realm and were made marquises. Hence the sworn covenant declared: 'None but the Liu clan shall be made king. Should anyone without merit or not appointed by the emperor be enfeoffed as marquis, all under heaven shall join in punishing him.' For this reason, when Empress Dowager Gao wished to make the Lü clan kings, Wang Ling openly opposed her in court; and when Emperor Jing was about to enfeoff the Wang clan, the Marquis of Xiu showed his displeasure openly. In the end, both were deposed and dismissed. Afterward, the marquessates of Bo Zhao, Dou Ying, and the Shangguan, Wei, and Huo clans were conferred on the basis of merit. The remaining enfeoffments were justified by the Spring and Autumn principle of honoring the empress's father, and the Greater Odes' ideal for the emperor's maternal uncle. The practice grew ever broader. For this reason, I have separated them out and narrated them apart.
