1
季布，楚人也，為任俠有名。 項籍使將兵，數窘漢王。 項籍滅，高祖購求布千金，敢有舍匿，罪三族。 布匿濮陽周氏，周氏曰：「漢求將軍急，跡且至臣家，能聽臣，臣敢進計； 即否，願先自剄。」 布許之。 乃髡鉗布，衣褐，置廣柳車中，并與其家僮數十人，之魯朱家所賣之。 朱家心知其季布也，買置田舍。 乃之雒陽見汝陰侯滕公，說曰：「季布何罪？ 臣各為其主用，職耳。 項氏臣豈可盡誅邪？ 今上始得天下，而以私怨求一人，何示不廣也！ 且以季布之賢，漢求之急如此，此不北走胡，南走越耳。 夫忌壯士以資敵國，此伍子胥所以鞭荊平之墓也。 君何不從容為上言之？」 滕公心知朱家大俠，意布匿其所，乃許諾。 侍間，果言如朱家指。 上乃赦布。 當是時，諸公皆多布能摧剛為柔，朱家亦以此名聞當世。 布召見，謝，拜郎中。
Ji Bu was a native of Chu and was widely known as a knight-errant who took up others' causes. Xiang Yu placed him in command of troops, and again and again he drove the King of Han into desperate positions. After Xiang Yu fell, the High Ancestor offered a bounty of a thousand catties of gold for Ji Bu; anyone who dared harbor him would be punished with extermination of three generations of his kin. Ji Bu took refuge with the Zhou family of Puyang. They told him, "The court is hunting you relentlessly; their trail will soon lead to my door. If you will trust me, I will put forward a plan; If not, I would rather slit my own throat first." Ji Bu agreed. They shaved Ji Bu's head, clamped him in the iron collar, dressed him in a laborer's hemp, hid him in a hearse-like cart with several dozen household retainers, and took the party to Zhu Jia of Lu to be "sold" as slaves. Zhu Jia recognized him as Ji Bu, bought him, and installed him on a farm. He then traveled to Luoyang to see the Marquis of Ru-yin, Lord Teng of Xia, and argued, "What wrong has Ji Bu done? Each minister served his own lord—that was nothing but duty. Surely you cannot put every minister who served the Xiangs to the sword? The Son of Heaven has only just won the realm; to hunt one man out of personal spite shows how narrow his magnanimity would appear. Besides, a man of Ji Bu's caliber, pressed this hard, will flee north to the Xiongnu or south to Yue—there is nowhere else for him to go. To drive a brave man into the arms of a rival is the very thing that made Wu Zixu exhume King Ping of Chu and flog his corpse. Why do you not find a suitable moment and put this to the emperor?" Lord Teng knew Zhu Jia for a famous knight-errant and guessed Ji Bu lay concealed with him; he agreed to speak. When a chance came, he repeated Zhu Jia's argument almost word for word. The emperor then granted Ji Bu a pardon. At the time, everyone admired Ji Bu for bending unyielding force into pliant survival, and Zhu Jia's fame spread through the realm for the same deed. Ji Bu was summoned to audience, expressed his gratitude, and was appointed a gentleman of the palace.
2
孝惠時，為中郎將。 單于嘗為書嫚呂太后，太后怒，召諸將議之。 上將軍樊噲曰：「臣願得十萬眾，橫行匈奴中。」 諸將皆阿呂太后，以噲言為然。 布曰：「樊噲可斬也。 夫以高帝兵三十餘萬，困於平城，噲時亦在其中。 今噲奈何以十萬眾橫行匈奴中，面謾！ 且秦以事胡，陳勝等起。 今瘡痍未瘳，噲又面諛，欲搖動天下。」 是時殿上皆恐，太后罷朝，遂不復議擊匈奴事。
Under Emperor Hui he served as a leader of the gentlemen-at-attendance of the palace. The Chanyu had once sent a letter grossly insulting Empress Dowager Lü; enraged, she called her generals in council. Fan Kuai, senior general, declared, "Give me a hundred thousand men and I will sweep through the heart of the Xiongnu steppe." The generals curried favor with the empress dowager and pronounced Fan Kuai right. Ji Bu said, "Fan Kuai deserves execution. The High Ancestor took more than three hundred thousand men to Pingcheng and was surrounded there—and Fan Kuai was in that army. How dare he now boast that a mere hundred thousand could ride roughshod over the Xiongnu—lying to our faces! Moreover, the Qin dynasty provoked the northern tribes and Chen Sheng's rebellion followed. The wounds of war are still raw, and Fan Kuai flatters you to your face, ready to unsettle the whole realm." A chill fell over the hall; the empress dowager ended the session, and the court never again debated an invasion of the Xiongnu.
3
布為河東守。 孝文時，人有言其賢，召欲以為御史大夫。 人又言其勇，使酒難近。 至，留邸一月，見罷。 布進曰：「臣待罪河東，陛下無故召臣，此人必有以臣欺陛下者。 今臣至，無所受事，罷去，此人必有毀臣者。 夫陛下以一人譽召臣，一人毀去臣，臣恐天下有識者聞之，有以窺陛下。」 上默然，慚曰：「河東吾股肱郡，故特召君耳。」 布之官。
Ji Bu was appointed governor of Hedong. Under Emperor Wen, someone praised his abilities; the emperor summoned him intending to name him imperial counselor. Others countered that he was fierce, given to drink, and impossible to work with. When he reached the capital he was lodged a month, then dismissed from audience without appointment. Ji Bu stepped forward and said, "I held office at Hedong awaiting fault; Your Majesty summoned me without cause—someone must have oversold me to you. Now that I am here with no duties given and am sent away, someone must have slandered me. If a single voice of praise can summon me and a single voice of blame dismiss me, thoughtful men everywhere will draw their own conclusions about Your Majesty's judgment." The emperor fell silent, then said with embarrassment, "Hedong is one of my vital commanderies; I called you chiefly for that reason." Ji Bu returned to his post.
4
辯士曹丘生數招權顧金錢，事貴人趙談等，與竇長君善。 布聞，寄書諫長君曰：「吾聞曹丘生非長者，勿與通。」 及曹丘生歸，欲得書請布。 竇長君曰：「季將軍不說足下，足下無往。」 固請書，遂行。 使人先發書，布果大怒，待曹丘。 曹丘至，則揖布曰：「楚人諺曰『得黃金百，不如得季布諾』，足下何以得此聲梁楚之間哉？ 且僕與足下俱楚人，使僕游揚足下名於天下，顧不美乎？ 何足下距僕之深也！」 布乃大說。 引入，留數月，為上客，厚送之。 布名所以益聞者，曹丘揚之也。
The rhetorician Cao Qiu Sheng traded on influence for cash, cultivated favorites such as Zhao Tan, and was close to Dou Zhang, the empress's brother. Ji Bu sent Lord Dou a letter of warning: "Cao Qiu Sheng is no man of honor; keep your distance." When Cao Qiu Sheng came home, he wanted a letter of introduction to Ji Bu. Lord Dou replied, "General Ji dislikes you; you had better not go." Cao pressed until he got the letter and set out. He sent the letter ahead; Ji Bu was furious and waited for Cao Qiu in a rage. When Cao Qiu arrived, he bowed and said, "Chu has a saying: 'A hundred catties of gold are worth less than Ji Bu's word'—how did you earn such renown across Liang and Chu? We are both men of Chu; let me spread your name through the empire—would that not serve you well? Why do you shut me out so completely!" Ji Bu was won over at once. He brought him in, kept him for months as an honored guest, and sent him away with rich gifts. It was Cao Qiu Sheng's praise that made Ji Bu's name ring louder still.
5
布弟季心氣蓋關中，遇人恭謹，為任俠，方數千里，士爭為死。 嘗殺人，亡吳，從爰絲匿，長事爰絲，弟畜灌夫、籍福之屬。 嘗為中司馬，中尉郅都不敢加。 少年多時時竊借其名以行。 當是時，季心以勇，布以諾，聞關中。
Ji Bu's younger brother Ji Xin dominated Guanzhong in sheer force of character; outwardly deferential, he acted the knight-errant over thousands of miles, and fighting men competed for the honor of dying in his service. After a killing he fled to Wu and hid with Yuan Ang, whom he served as an elder patron while treating Guan Fu, Ji Fu, and their like as junior companions. He rose to central marshal in the guard; even Zhi Du, the dreaded commandant of the capital, did not dare touch him. Young blades constantly traded on his name behind his back. In those days Ji Xin was famed in Guanzhong for courage, Ji Bu for keeping his word.
6
布母弟丁公，為項羽將，逐窘高祖彭城西。 短兵接，漢王急，顧謂丁公曰：「兩賢豈相厄哉！」 丁公引兵而還。 及項王滅，丁公謁見高祖，以丁公徇軍中，曰：「丁公為項王臣不忠，使項王失天下者也。」 遂斬之，曰：「使後為人臣無傚丁公也！」
Ji Bu's uncle Ding Gong, a general under Xiang Yu, harried the High Ancestor west of Pengcheng. In the melee the King of Han was hard pressed; wheeling his horse he cried to Ding Gong, "Must two able men try to destroy each other?" Ding Gong withdrew his troops. After Xiang Yu fell, Ding Gong presented himself to the High Ancestor, who had him paraded before the host and declared, "Ding Gong served the Hegemon-King without loyalty; he is the man who cost Xiang Yu the empire." He had him executed, adding, "Let no minister ever take Ding Gong as his model!"
7
欒布，梁人也。 彭越為家人時，嘗與布游，窮困，賣庸於齊，為酒家保。 數歲別去，而布為人所略，賣為奴於燕。 為其主家報仇，燕將臧荼舉以為都尉。 荼為燕王，布為將。 及荼反，漢擊燕，虜布。 梁王彭越聞之，乃言上，請贖布為梁大夫。 使於齊，未反，漢召彭越責以謀反，夷三族，梟首雒陽，下詔有收視者輒捕之。 布還，奏事彭越頭下，祠而哭之。 吏捕以聞。 上召布罵曰：「若與彭越反邪？ 吾禁人勿收，若獨祠而哭之，與反明矣。 趣亨之。」 方提趨湯，顧曰：「願一言而死。」 上曰：「何言？」 布曰：「方上之困彭城，敗滎陽、成皋間，項王所以不能遂西，徒以彭王居梁地，與漢合從苦楚也。 當是之時，彭王壹顧，與楚則漢破，與漢則楚破。 且垓下之會，微彭王，項氏不亡。 天下已定，彭王剖符受封，亦欲傳之萬世。 今漢壹徵兵於梁，彭王病不行，而疑以為反。 反形未見，以苛細誅之，臣恐功臣人人自危也。 今彭王已死，臣生不如死，請就亨。」 上乃釋布，拜為都尉。
Luan Bu was a native of Liang. When Peng Yue was still a commoner, he and Luan Bu wandered together; penniless, Luan hired out his labor in Qi and stood surety for a wine merchant. After some years they parted, and Luan Bu was kidnapped and sold into slavery in Yan. He avenged his master's family; the Yan general Zang Tu, impressed, appointed him a commandant. When Zang Tu became King of Yan, Luan Bu served as his general. When Zang Tu rebelled, Han attacked Yan and took Luan Bu prisoner. King Peng Yue of Liang petitioned the emperor to ransom him and appoint him a grandee of Liang. Luan Bu was on a mission to Qi when the court summoned Peng Yue, accused him of treason, wiped out three generations of his kin, and exposed his head in Luoyang; an edict threatened arrest for anyone who dared mourn him. Luan Bu returned, presented his report beneath Peng Yue's impaled head, and offered sacrifice with weeping. The authorities seized him and reported to the throne. The emperor had Luan Bu brought in and shouted, "Were you in league with Peng Yue? I forbade anyone to bury him; you alone mourned him at sacrifice—your guilt is plain as rebellion. Boil him at once." As they dragged him toward the cauldron, he looked back and said, "Let me speak one sentence before I die." The emperor asked, "What is it?" Luan Bu said, "When you were beleaguered at Pengcheng and driven back between Xingyang and Chenggao, the only reason Xiang Yu failed to push west was that the King of Peng held Liang and kept Han and Liang in alliance against Chu. At that moment a single shift of Peng Yue's weight—if he had thrown in with Chu, Han would have shattered; if with Han, Chu would have shattered. Even at Gaixia, without the King of Peng the house of Xiang would not have fallen. Once the realm was settled, the King of Peng received his tally and fief like any other founder, expecting his line to endure forever. Now a single levy from Liang finds him too ill to march, and he is branded a traitor on suspicion alone. No act of rebellion appeared; to kill him on petty pretexts will make every man who helped you win the throne fear for his life. Peng Yue is dead; I would rather die with him—send me to the cauldron." The emperor freed him and named him a commandant.
8
孝文時，為燕相，至將軍。 布稱曰：「窮困不能辱身，非人也； 富貴不能快意，非賢也。」 於是嘗有德，厚報之； 有怨，必以法滅之。 吳楚反時，以功封為鄃侯，復為燕相。 燕齊之間皆為立社，號曰欒公社。
Under Emperor Wen he rose from chancellor of Yan to general. Luan Bu used to say, "In hardship, if you cannot bear indignity to your person, you are less than human; in wealth and rank, if you cannot live as your heart demands, you are no worthy man." Those who had done him a kindness he repaid lavishly; those who had wronged him he destroyed to the letter of the law. During the rebellion of Wu and Chu he earned the title Marquis of Ju and returned as chancellor of Yan. Between Yan and Qi the people founded shrines to him and called them the altars of Duke Luan.
9
布薨，子賁嗣侯，孝武時坐為太常犧牲不如令，國除。
When Luan Bu died, his son Ben inherited the marquisate; under Emperor Wu the fief was stripped because, as grand master of ceremonies, Ben's sacrificial animals failed to meet statute.
10
田叔，趙陘城人也。 其先，齊田氏也。 叔好劍，學黃老術於樂鉅公。 為人廉直，喜任俠。 游諸公，趙人舉之趙相趙午，言之趙王張敖，以為郎中。 數歲，趙王賢之，未及遷。
Tian Shu was a native of Xingcheng in Zhao. His family sprang from the Tian house of Qi. He loved fencing and studied Huang-Lao doctrine under Master Yue Ju. He was scrupulous and blunt-spoken, and delighted in chivalrous deeds. He moved among the great families until men of Zhao recommended him to Chancellor Zhao Wu, who brought him to the notice of King Zhang Ao of Zhao and had him appointed a gentleman of the palace. For several years the king admired him but had not yet promoted him.
11
會趙午、貫高等謀弒上，事發覺，漢下詔捕趙王及群臣反者。 趙有敢隨王，罪三族。 唯田叔、孟舒等十餘人赭衣自髡鉗，隨王至長安。 趙王敖事白，得出，廢王為宣平侯，乃進言叔等十人。 上召見，與語，漢廷臣無能出其右者。 上說，盡拜為郡守、諸侯相。 叔為漢中守十餘年。
Then Zhao Wu, Guan Gao, and others plotted regicide; when the plot was uncovered, Han issued warrants for King Zhang of Zhao and every minister implicated. In Zhao, anyone who dared follow the king would suffer extermination of three clans. Only Tian Shu, Meng Shu, and a dozen others shaved their heads, donned the convict collar and crimson robes, and followed their king to Chang'an. When the case against King Zhang Ao was cleared, he was released, demoted from kingship to Marquis of Xuanping, and then recommended Tian Shu and the other nine men to the throne. The emperor received them in audience and, speaking with each in turn, found that none of his ministers could match their caliber. Delighted, he appointed them all as governors of commanderies or chancellors to the feudal kings. Tian Shu governed Hanzhong for more than a decade.
12
孝文帝初立，召叔問曰：「公知天下長者乎？」 對曰：「臣何足以知之！」 上曰：「公長者，宜知之。」 叔頓首曰：「故雲中守孟舒，長者也。」 是時孟舒坐虜大入雲中免。 上曰：「先帝置孟舒雲中十餘年矣，虜常一入，孟舒不能堅守，無故士卒戰死者數百人。 長者固殺人乎？」 叔叩頭曰：「夫貫高等謀反，天子下明詔，趙有敢隨張王者罪三族，然孟舒自髡鉗，隨張王，以身死之，豈自知為雲中守哉！ 漢與楚相距，士卒罷敝，而匈奴冒頓新服北夷，來為邊寇，孟舒知士卒罷敝，不忍出言，士爭臨城死敵，如子為父，以故死者數百人，孟舒豈敺之哉！ 是乃孟舒所以為長者。」 於是上曰：「賢哉孟舒！」 復召以為雲中守。
Early in Emperor Wen's reign he summoned Tian Shu and asked, "Sir, whom do you count among the most honorable men in the realm?" He answered, "Your servant is far too slight a man to presume to judge that." The emperor said, "You are yourself a man of substance; you ought to know." Tian Shu kowtowed and said, "The former governor of Yunzhong, Meng Shu, is such a man." Meng Shu had just been removed from office for allowing the Xiongnu to overrun Yunzhong. The emperor said, "The late emperor kept Meng Shu at Yunzhong for over ten years; when the barbarians broke in, he failed to hold the line, and hundreds of soldiers died in battle for nothing. Is that what we call a man of honor—to let hundreds perish?" Tian Shu kowtowed and said, "When Guan Gao and his fellows plotted treason, the Son of Heaven issued an edict that anyone in Zhao who followed King Zhang would suffer extermination of three generations—yet Meng Shu shaved his head, clamped on the collar, and followed the king to share his fate, with no thought that he would ever govern Yunzhong! Han and Chu were locked in war, the troops were spent, and Modu had just brought the northern tribes to heel and turned them against our borders. Meng Shu saw how worn his men were and could not bring himself to order them to fight; they climbed the walls and died like sons defending a father, and hundreds fell—not because Meng Shu drove them to it! That is what makes Meng Shu a true man of honor." The emperor exclaimed, "Meng Shu is a worthy man indeed!" He was summoned back and reappointed governor of Yunzhong.
13
後數歲，叔坐法失官。 梁孝王使人殺漢議臣爰盎，景帝召叔案梁，具得其事。 還報，上曰：「梁有之乎？」 對曰：「有之。」 「事安在？」 叔曰：「上無以梁事為問也。 今梁王不伏誅，是廢漢法也； 如其伏誅，太后食不甘味，臥不安席，此憂在陛下。」 於是上大賢之，以為魯相。
Some years later Tian Shu lost his post for a legal infraction. When King Xiao of Liang had the Han minister Yuan Ang murdered, Emperor Jing sent Tian Shu to investigate the kingdom of Liang, and he uncovered the whole plot. On his return the emperor asked, "Did Liang really do this?" He answered, "It did." "Where is the evidence?" Tian Shu said, "Your Majesty should not press me about the affair in Liang. If the king of Liang goes unpunished, the laws of Han are void; if he is executed, the empress dowager will lose her appetite and her sleep, and Your Majesty will bear that grief." The emperor admired him greatly and appointed him chancellor to the king of Lu.
14
相初至官，民以王取其財物自言者百餘人。 叔取其渠率二千人笞，怒之曰：「王非汝主邪？ 何敢自言主！」 魯王聞之，大慚，發中府錢，使相償之。 相曰：「王自使人償之，不爾，是王為惡而相為善也。」
As soon as he took office, more than a hundred commoners petitioned that the king had seized their property. Tian Shu seized some two thousand ringleaders from the crowd and had them flogged, shouting, "Is the king not your sovereign? How dare you bring accusations against your own sovereign!" The king of Lu was deeply shamed; he drew funds from the inner treasury and told the chancellor to pay the claimants. The chancellor replied, "Let Your Highness send your own men to make restitution; otherwise it will look as though the king did wrong and the chancellor played the hero."
15
魯王好獵，相常從入苑中，王輒休相就館。 相常暴坐苑外，終不休，曰：「吾王暴露，獨何為舍？」 王以故不大出遊。
The king of Lu loved the chase; the chancellor always rode with him into the hunting park, but the king would dismiss him to the lodge. Tian Shu would sit in the open outside the enclosure and refuse to leave, saying, "If my king endures wind and weather, why should I alone seek shelter?" For that reason the king rarely went out on pleasure hunts.
16
數年以官卒，魯以百金祠，少子仁不受，曰：「義不傷先人名。」
He died in office some years later; Lu offered a hundred catties of gold for his memorial, but his youngest son Tian Ren refused it, saying, "Duty forbids me to trade on my father's good name."
17
仁以壯勇為衛將軍舍人，數從擊匈奴。 衛將軍進言仁為郎中，至二千石、丞相長史，失官。 後使刺三河，還，奏事稱意，拜為京輔都尉。 月餘，遷司直。 數歲，戾太子舉兵，仁部閉城門，令太子得亡，坐縱反者族。
Tian Ren won a place as a retainer to General Wei Qing through his courage and rode with him on repeated campaigns against the Xiongnu. Wei Qing recommended him until he rose to gentleman of the palace, then to two thousand shi and chief clerk to the chancellor, after which he lost his post. He was later dispatched to inspect the Three Rivers region; his report pleased the emperor, and he was named commandant of the capital area. A little over a month later he was promoted to director of integrity. Some years later, when Crown Prince Ju rose in arms, Tian Ren held command of the gates and manipulated their closure so that the heir could escape; for that leniency toward a rebel his entire clan was put to death.
18
贊曰：以項羽之氣，而季布以勇顯名楚，身履軍搴旗者數矣，可謂壯士。 及至困厄奴僇，苟活而不變，何也？ 彼自負其材，受辱不羞，欲有所用其未足也，故終為漢名將。 賢者誠重其死。 夫婢妾賤人，感概而自殺，非能勇也，其畫無俚之至耳。 欒布哭彭越，田叔隨張敖，赴死如歸，彼誠知所處，雖古烈士，何以加哉！
The historian concludes: In an age ruled by Xiang Yu's tempest, Ji Bu made a name in Chu for sheer nerve; again and again he fought through the ranks and tore down enemy standards—a man in the true mold of the warrior. Yet when reduced to chains and public shame, they clung to a humiliating life without yielding their purpose—why? They trusted their own abilities; they bore disgrace without flinching because they knew they still had more to give, and so they rose at last to become celebrated commanders of Han. Men of true worth do not throw their lives away lightly. When bondmaids and concubines slash their wrists in a surge of temper, that is not courage; their thinking is as empty as it can be. Luan Bu mourned Peng Yue at the risk of boiling oil; Tian Shu followed Zhang Ao in fetters as calmly as if he were going home—they knew exactly what they were about.
