1
季布，楚人也，為任俠有名。 項籍使將兵，數窘漢王。 項籍滅，高祖購求布千金，敢有舍匿，罪三族。 布匿濮陽周氏，周氏曰：「漢求將軍急，跡且至臣家，能聽臣，臣敢進計； 即否，願先自剄。」 布許之。 乃髡鉗布，衣褐，置廣柳車中，并與其家僮數十人，之魯朱家所賣之。 朱家心知其季布也，買置田舍。 乃之雒陽見汝陰侯滕公，說曰：「季布何罪？ 臣各為其主用，職耳。 項氏臣豈可盡誅邪？ 今上始得天下，而以私怨求一人，何示不廣也！ 且以季布之賢，漢求之急如此，此不北走胡，南走越耳。 夫忌壯士以資敵國，此伍子胥所以鞭荊平之墓也。 君何不從容為上言之？」 滕公心知朱家大俠，意布匿其所，乃許諾。 侍間，果言如朱家指。 上乃赦布。 當是時，諸公皆多布能摧剛為柔，朱家亦以此名聞當世。 布召見，謝，拜郎中。
Ji Bu, a native of Chu, was celebrated as a knight-errant who lived by a strict code of honor. Xiang Ji put him in command of troops, and he several times drove the King of Han into desperate straits. After Xiang Ji fell, Gaozu offered a thousand pieces of gold for Ji Bu's capture and decreed that anyone who dared shelter him would suffer the punishment of kin extermination to the third degree. Ji Bu hid with the Zhou family of Puyang. They said to him, "The Han court is hunting you relentlessly; their agents will soon trace you here. If you will hear me out, I have a plan to offer;" "If you refuse, I would rather cut my own throat first." Ji Bu agreed. They shaved Ji Bu's head, fitted him with the convict's collar, dressed him in a laborer's hemp robe, stowed him in a freight cart among dozens of household slaves, and drove him to Zhu Jia's estate in Lu to be sold off as a slave. Zhu Jia recognized him at once as Ji Bu, purchased him, and lodged him on a country farm. He then traveled to Luoyang to see the Marquis of Ruyin, Lord Teng, and argued, "What wrong has Ji Bu committed?" Each man serves his own lord as duty requires—that is all there is to it. Surely you cannot execute every minister who ever served the house of Xiang. The emperor has only just won the empire; to hound one man out of private spite hardly shows a generous breadth of mind. A man of Ji Bu's caliber, pressed this hard by the Han, will simply flee north to the steppe peoples or south into the Yue lands. To drive bold men into the arms of rival powers out of fear—think of why Wu Zixu lashed the grave of King Ping of Chu. Why not find a tactful moment to put this to the emperor yourself?" Lord Teng knew Zhu Jia by reputation as a great knight-errant and guessed that Ji Bu lay concealed there; he agreed to plead his case. He waited for a suitable opening and repeated Zhu Jia's arguments to the throne. The emperor then pardoned Ji Bu. At the time, leading men admired Ji Bu for turning inflexible danger into a yielding escape, and Zhu Jia's name rang through the age for what he had done. Ji Bu was summoned to court, expressed his gratitude, and was appointed a gentleman-attendant.
2
孝惠時，為中郎將。 單于嘗為書嫚呂太后，太后怒，召諸將議之。 上將軍樊噲曰：「臣願得十萬眾，橫行匈奴中。」 諸將皆阿呂太后，以噲言為然。 布曰：「樊噲可斬也。 夫以高帝兵三十餘萬，困於平城，噲時亦在其中。 今噲奈何以十萬眾橫行匈奴中，面謾！ 且秦以事胡，陳勝等起。 今瘡痍未瘳，噲又面諛，欲搖動天下。」 是時殿上皆恐，太后罷朝，遂不復議擊匈奴事。
Under Emperor Xiaohui he rose to the rank of Commandant of the Palace Gentlemen. The Xiongnu chanyu had once sent a letter grossly insulting Empress Dowager Lü. Enraged, she convened her generals to discuss a military response. Fan Kuai, the senior general, declared, "Give me a hundred thousand men and I will sweep through the heart of the Xiongnu domain." The generals all flattered the empress dowager and agreed that Fan Kuai was right. Ji Bu said, "Fan Kuai deserves execution. Gaozu once led more than three hundred thousand men and was still trapped at Pingcheng; Fan Kuai was in that army. How can he now claim he will rampage through Xiongnu territory with a mere hundred thousand? He is lying to your faces! Besides, the Qin exhausted the realm on campaigns against the northern tribes, and men like Chen Sheng rose in rebellion. Our wounds from war are still raw, and Fan Kuai would flatter you to your face and risk unsettling the whole empire." A chill fell over the hall; the empress dowager adjourned court, and the proposal to attack the Xiongnu was never revived.
3
布為河東守。 孝文時，人有言其賢，召欲以為御史大夫。 人又言其勇，使酒難近。 至，留邸一月，見罷。 布進曰：「臣待罪河東，陛下無故召臣，此人必有以臣欺陛下者。 今臣至，無所受事，罷去，此人必有毀臣者。 夫陛下以一人譽召臣，一人毀去臣，臣恐天下有識者聞之，有以窺陛下。」 上默然，慚曰：「河東吾股肱郡，故特召君耳。」 布之官。
Ji Bu was appointed governor of Hedong. Under Emperor Xiaowen, someone praised his abilities; the emperor summoned him with the intention of appointing him imperial counselor. Others warned that he was headstrong and surly when drunk, and hard to work with at court. When he arrived, he was kept at the official lodge for a month and then dismissed without receiving an appointment. Ji Bu stepped forward and said, "I stand under sentence of duty at Hedong; Your Majesty summoned me without apparent reason—someone must have used me to mislead you." Now that I have come, I have been given no assignment and am sent away—someone must be slandering me behind my back." If a single man's praise brings me to court and a single man's slander sends me away, thoughtful men everywhere will draw their own conclusions about how Your Majesty judges men." The emperor sat in silence, then said with embarrassment, "Hedong is a pillar of the realm; I summoned you for that reason alone." Ji Bu returned to his post.
4
辯士曹丘生數招權顧金錢，事貴人趙談等，與竇長君善。 布聞，寄書諫長君曰：「吾聞曹丘生非長者，勿與通。」 及曹丘生歸，欲得書請布。 竇長君曰：「季將軍不說足下，足下無往。」 固請書，遂行。 使人先發書，布果大怒，待曹丘。 曹丘至，則揖布曰：「楚人諺曰『得黃金百，不如得季布諾』，足下何以得此聲梁楚之間哉？ 且僕與足下俱楚人，使僕游揚足下名於天下，顧不美乎？ 何足下距僕之深也！」 布乃大說。 引入，留數月，為上客，厚送之。 布名所以益聞者，曹丘揚之也。
Cao Qiusheng, a persuasive talker, repeatedly traded on influence for cash, attached himself to powerful men such as Zhao Tan, and was on close terms with Dou Changjun. When Ji Bu heard of it, he wrote Dou Changjun a warning: "Cao Qiusheng is no man of integrity. Have nothing to do with him." When Cao Qiusheng returned home, he asked for a letter of introduction to Ji Bu. Dou Changjun replied, "General Ji has no liking for you. You had better not go." Cao pressed his request until he obtained the letter and set out. He had his letter of introduction sent ahead; Ji Bu was furious and waited for Cao Qiusheng. When Cao Qiusheng arrived, he bowed to Ji Bu and said, "The people of Chu say, 'A hundred pounds of gold cannot buy Ji Bu's word.' How did you earn such a name between Liang and Chu?" We are both men of Chu; if I speak well of you to the world, would that not serve you handsomely?" Yet you have shut me out so coldly!" Ji Bu was completely won over. He brought him inside, kept him for months as an honored guest, and sent him away with rich gifts. It was Cao Qiusheng's praise that spread Ji Bu's fame still farther.
5
布弟季心氣蓋關中，遇人恭謹，為任俠，方數千里，士爭為死。 嘗殺人，亡吳，從爰絲匿，長事爰絲，弟畜灌夫、籍福之屬。 嘗為中司馬，中尉郅都不敢加。 少年多時時竊借其名以行。 當是時，季心以勇，布以諾，聞關中。
Ji Bu's younger brother Ji Xin dominated Guanzhong in sheer dash; though unfailingly courteous in meeting others, he was a knight-errant whose reputation carried for thousands of li, and fighting men vied to die for him. He once killed a man and fled to Wu, where he hid with Yuan Ang, served Yuan as an elder brother would be served, and treated the likes of Guan Fu and Ji Fu as younger companions. When he served as an aide in the central command, even the dreaded Zhi Du dared not touch him. Young blades often falsely invoked his name. At the time Ji Xin was known for valor and Ji Bu for keeping his word; both names filled Guanzhong.
6
布母弟丁公，為項羽將，逐窘高祖彭城西。 短兵接，漢王急，顧謂丁公曰：「兩賢豈相厄哉！」 丁公引兵而還。 及項王滅，丁公謁見高祖，以丁公徇軍中，曰：「丁公為項王臣不忠，使項王失天下者也。」 遂斬之，曰：「使後為人臣無傚丁公也！」
Ji Bu's younger uterine brother, Lord Ding, served as a general under Xiang Yu and drove Gaozu into desperate straits west of Pengcheng. In the melee the King of Han was hard pressed; turning to Lord Ding he cried, "Surely two able men need not ruin each other!" Lord Ding withdrew his troops and let him go. After Xiang Yu fell, Lord Ding presented himself to Gaozu. Gaozu had him paraded before the troops and declared, "Lord Ding was a faithless minister to the King of Xiang; he is the man who cost Xiang Yu the empire." He had him beheaded, saying, "Let no minister hereafter take Lord Ding as his model!"
7
欒布，梁人也。 彭越為家人時，嘗與布游，窮困，賣庸於齊，為酒家保。 數歲別去，而布為人所略，賣為奴於燕。 為其主家報仇，燕將臧荼舉以為都尉。 荼為燕王，布為將。 及荼反，漢擊燕，虜布。 梁王彭越聞之，乃言上，請贖布為梁大夫。 使於齊，未反，漢召彭越責以謀反，夷三族，梟首雒陽，下詔有收視者輒捕之。 布還，奏事彭越頭下，祠而哭之。 吏捕以聞。 上召布罵曰：「若與彭越反邪？ 吾禁人勿收，若獨祠而哭之，與反明矣。 趣亨之。」 方提趨湯，顧曰：「願一言而死。」 上曰：「何言？」 布曰：「方上之困彭城，敗滎陽、成皋間，項王所以不能遂西，徒以彭王居梁地，與漢合從苦楚也。 當是之時，彭王壹顧，與楚則漢破，與漢則楚破。 且垓下之會，微彭王，項氏不亡。 天下已定，彭王剖符受封，亦欲傳之萬世。 今漢壹徵兵於梁，彭王病不行，而疑以為反。 反形未見，以苛細誅之，臣恐功臣人人自危也。 今彭王已死，臣生不如死，請就亨。」 上乃釋布，拜為都尉。
Luan Bu was a native of Liang. When Peng Yue was still a commoner, he had roamed with Luan Bu in hardship; destitute, they hired out as laborers in Qi, and Luan Bu worked as a bondservant in a wineshop. After some years they parted, and Luan Bu was kidnapped and sold into slavery in Yan. He avenged his master's family on their enemies, and the Yan general Zang Tu thereupon appointed him a commandant. When Zang Tu became King of Yan, Luan Bu served as one of his generals. When Zang Tu rebelled, the Han attacked Yan and took Luan Bu prisoner. King Peng Yue of Liang heard of it and petitioned the throne to ransom Luan Bu and appoint him a counselor of Liang. He was on a mission to Qi and had not returned when the Han summoned Peng Yue, charged him with treason, wiped out three generations of his kin, exposed his head at Luoyang, and decreed that anyone who dared mourn or bury him would be arrested at once. When Luan Bu returned, he presented his report beneath Peng Yue's impaled head, then offered sacrifice and wept for his lord. The authorities seized him and reported the matter. The emperor summoned Luan Bu and shouted, "Were you in league with Peng Yue?" I forbade anyone to bury him; you alone mourned him with sacrifice and tears—your guilt is plain as rebellion." "Boil him at once." As they dragged him toward the boiling cauldron, he turned and said, "Let me speak one sentence before I die." The emperor asked, "What is it?" Luan Bu said, "When Your Majesty was trapped at Pengcheng and driven back between Xingyang and Chenggao, the reason Xiang Yu could not push west was that the King of Peng held Liang and allied with Han to pin Chu in a vise." In those days a single shift of Peng Yue's weight—if he had thrown in with Chu, Han would have shattered; if with Han, Chu would have shattered." At Gaixia, had it not been for the King of Peng, the house of Xiang would not have fallen." Once the realm was settled, the King of Peng received his investiture on split tallies like any other lord; he meant his line to endure for ages." Now a single levy of troops from Liang found the king too ill to march, and on that slender ground he was branded a traitor." No act of rebellion had appeared; to put him to death on petty pretexts will make every man who helped win the empire fear for his life." Peng Yue is dead; for me to live on would be worse than death. Send me to the cauldron." The emperor then spared Luan Bu and appointed him a commandant.
8
孝文時，為燕相，至將軍。 布稱曰：「窮困不能辱身，非人也； 富貴不能快意，非賢也。」 於是嘗有德，厚報之； 有怨，必以法滅之。 吳楚反時，以功封為鄃侯，復為燕相。 燕齊之間皆為立社，號曰欒公社。
Under Emperor Xiaowen he served as chancellor of Yan and rose to the rank of general. Luan Bu used to say, "In poverty, if you cannot endure indignity to the body, you are not a true man;" "and in wealth and rank, if you cannot live as your heart dictates, you are no worthy man." Those who had done him a kindness he repaid with lavish generosity; those who had wronged him he destroyed to the letter of the law. During the rebellion of Wu and Chu he was enfeoffed as Marquis of Yu for his service and again became chancellor of Yan. Between Yan and Qi the people founded shrines to him and called them the altars of Duke Luan.
9
布薨，子賁嗣侯，孝武時坐為太常犧牲不如令，國除。
When Luan Bu died, his son Ben inherited the marquisate; under Emperor Wu he lost the fief for supplying sacrificial animals to the Grand Master of Ceremonies that failed to meet regulations.
10
田叔，趙陘城人也。 其先，齊田氏也。 叔好劍，學黃老術於樂鉅公。 為人廉直，喜任俠。 游諸公，趙人舉之趙相趙午，言之趙王張敖，以為郎中。 數歲，趙王賢之，未及遷。
Tian Shu was a native of Xingcheng in Zhao. His family traced its descent to the Tian house of Qi. Tian Shu loved swordsmanship and studied Huang-Lao doctrine under Master Yue Ju. He was scrupulous and upright, and delighted in the code of the knight-errant. He moved among the great families of the capital until men of Zhao recommended him to the Zhao chancellor Zhao Wu, who in turn spoke to King Zhang Ao of Zhao, who appointed him a gentleman-attendant. For several years the king of Zhao admired his abilities but had not yet promoted him.
11
會趙午、貫高等謀弒上，事發覺，漢下詔捕趙王及群臣反者。 趙有敢隨王，罪三族。 唯田叔、孟舒等十餘人赭衣自髡鉗，隨王至長安。 趙王敖事白，得出，廢王為宣平侯，乃進言叔等十人。 上召見，與語，漢廷臣無能出其右者。 上說，盡拜為郡守、諸侯相。 叔為漢中守十餘年。
Then Zhao Wu, Guan Gao, and others plotted regicide; when the plot was uncovered, the Han court ordered the arrest of the king of Zhao and every minister implicated in treason. In Zhao, anyone who dared follow the king would suffer kin extermination to the third degree. Only Tian Shu, Meng Shu, and a dozen others shaved their heads, donned the convict's collar and russet robes, and followed their king to Chang'an. When King Zhang Ao's case was cleared, he was released, stripped of his kingship, and enfeoffed as Marquis of Xuanping; he then recommended Tian Shu and the other nine men to the throne. The emperor received them in audience and spoke with them; not one minister at the Han court proved their equal. Pleased, the emperor appointed them all as governors of commanderies or chancellors to feudal princes. Tian Shu served as governor of Hanzhong for more than a decade.
12
孝文帝初立，召叔問曰：「公知天下長者乎？」 對曰：「臣何足以知之！」 上曰：「公長者，宜知之。」 叔頓首曰：「故雲中守孟舒，長者也。」 是時孟舒坐虜大入雲中免。 上曰：「先帝置孟舒雲中十餘年矣，虜常一入，孟舒不能堅守，無故士卒戰死者數百人。 長者固殺人乎？」 叔叩頭曰：「夫貫高等謀反，天子下明詔，趙有敢隨張王者罪三族，然孟舒自髡鉗，隨張王，以身死之，豈自知為雲中守哉！ 漢與楚相距，士卒罷敝，而匈奴冒頓新服北夷，來為邊寇，孟舒知士卒罷敝，不忍出言，士爭臨城死敵，如子為父，以故死者數百人，孟舒豈敺之哉！ 是乃孟舒所以為長者。」 於是上曰：「賢哉孟舒！」 復召以為雲中守。
Soon after Emperor Wen took the throne, he summoned Tian Shu and asked, "Do you know who the most worthy and steadfast men in the realm are?" Tian Shu replied, "How could a man like me presume to judge such things?" The emperor said, "You are yourself a man of integrity; you should know." Tian Shu kowtowed and said, "Meng Shu, the former governor of Yunzhong, is such a man." Meng Shu had just been dismissed because the barbarians had overrun Yunzhong on his watch. The emperor said, "The late emperor kept Meng Shu at Yunzhong for over ten years. Whenever the raiders broke in, he failed to hold the line, and hundreds of soldiers died in battle for no good reason. Is that how a 'worthy man' behaves—sending men to their deaths?" Tian Shu kowtowed and said, "When Guan Gao and the others plotted treason, the Son of Heaven issued a clear edict: in Zhao, anyone who followed King Zhang would suffer kin extermination to the third degree. Yet Meng Shu shaved his head, put on the convict's collar, and followed King Zhang, ready to die at his side. How could he have dreamed he would one day govern Yunzhong! Han and Chu were locked in stalemate; the troops were worn out, while Modu of the Xiongnu had just subdued the northern tribes and was raiding the frontier. Meng Shu saw how spent his men were and could not bring himself to order them out; they vied to man the walls and die like sons for a father, which is why hundreds fell—Meng Shu did not drive them to it! That is precisely why Meng Shu is a true man of worth." The emperor exclaimed, "Meng Shu is indeed a worthy man!" He summoned Meng Shu back and reappointed him governor of Yunzhong.
13
後數歲，叔坐法失官。 梁孝王使人殺漢議臣爰盎，景帝召叔案梁，具得其事。 還報，上曰：「梁有之乎？」 對曰：「有之。」 「事安在？」 叔曰：「上無以梁事為問也。 今梁王不伏誅，是廢漢法也； 如其伏誅，太后食不甘味，臥不安席，此憂在陛下。」 於是上大賢之，以為魯相。
Some years later Tian Shu lost his post for a legal infraction. King Xiao of Liang had had the Han councilor Yuan Ang murdered; Emperor Jing summoned Tian Shu to investigate the affair in Liang, and he uncovered the full truth. On his return he reported. The emperor asked, "Was Liang involved?" Tian Shu answered, "Yes." "Where is the proof?" Tian Shu said, "Your Majesty should not press the matter of Liang. If the king of Liang is not punished, the law of the Han is set aside; if he is executed, the empress dowager will lose her appetite and her sleep—the burden of that grief will fall on Your Majesty." The emperor admired his judgment and appointed him chancellor to the king of Lu.
14
相初至官，民以王取其財物自言者百餘人。 叔取其渠率二千人笞，怒之曰：「王非汝主邪？ 何敢自言主！」 魯王聞之，大慚，發中府錢，使相償之。 相曰：「王自使人償之，不爾，是王為惡而相為善也。」
Soon after the new chancellor took office, more than a hundred commoners came forward claiming the king had seized their property. Tian Shu had their ringleaders—some two thousand men—flogged and rebuked them: "Is the king not your sovereign? How dare you lodge formal accusations against your own lord?" The king of Lu was deeply shamed; he drew funds from the inner treasury and told the chancellor to make restitution. The chancellor replied, "Let Your Majesty's own agents pay them back; otherwise it will look as though the king did wrong and the chancellor played the hero."
15
魯王好獵，相常從入苑中，王輒休相就館。 相常暴坐苑外，終不休，曰：「吾王暴露，獨何為舍？」 王以故不大出遊。
The king of Lu loved the chase; the chancellor often accompanied him into the hunting park, and the king would dismiss him to the guest quarters to rest. The chancellor would sit in the open outside the park and refuse to leave, saying, "While my king endures wind and weather, why should I take shelter?" After that the king rarely went out on long hunting expeditions.
16
數年以官卒，魯以百金祠，少子仁不受，曰：「義不傷先人名。」
Some years later he died in office. The state of Lu offered a hundred catties of gold for his funeral rites, but his youngest son, Ren, refused it, saying, "Duty forbids me to tarnish my father's name."
17
仁以壯勇為衛將軍舍人，數從擊匈奴。 衛將軍進言仁為郎中，至二千石、丞相長史，失官。 後使刺三河，還，奏事稱意，拜為京輔都尉。 月餘，遷司直。 數歲，戾太子舉兵，仁部閉城門，令太子得亡，坐縱反者族。
Ren, bold and brave, became a retainer of the General of the Guards and repeatedly accompanied him on campaigns against the Xiongnu. The general recommended him for appointment as a gentleman-attendant; he rose to the rank of two thousand piculs as chief clerk to the chancellor, then lost his post. Later he was dispatched to inspect the Three He region; on his return his report pleased the throne, and he was appointed commandant of the capital area. A little over a month later he was promoted to director of upright conduct. Some years later, when the heir apparent Liu Ju mutinied, Tian Ren, whose detachment controlled the city gates, managed their closure in such a way that the heir broke out of the capital; he was convicted of letting a rebel escape and his entire clan was executed.
18
贊曰：以項羽之氣，而季布以勇顯名楚，身履軍搴旗者數矣，可謂壯士。 及至困厄奴僇，苟活而不變，何也？ 彼自負其材，受辱不羞，欲有所用其未足也，故終為漢名將。 賢者誠重其死。 夫婢妾賤人，感概而自殺，非能勇也，其畫無俚之至耳。 欒布哭彭越，田叔隨張敖，赴死如歸，彼誠知所處，雖古烈士，何以加哉！
The appraisal states: In an age dominated by Xiang Yu's dash, Ji Bu won fame in Chu for his courage; he repeatedly took the field and tore enemy banners from their staffs—a true stalwart. Yet when he fell to the depths, shamed like a slave, he clung to life without yielding his principles—why was that? He trusted in his own abilities; he could endure disgrace without losing heart because he still had more to give the world, and so he ended his career as a celebrated general of the Han. A worthy man truly weighs the worth of his life. Maids and concubines, the meanest sort, may work themselves into a passion and take their own lives; that is not courage but the shallowest, most witless impulse. Luan Bu mourned Peng Yue at the risk of his life; Tian Shu followed Zhang Ao to the edge of death as calmly as if going home. They knew exactly where they stood; the boldest heroes of antiquity could not surpass them.
