Volume 37: Ji Bu, Luan Bu and Tian Shu

1
季布，楚人也，為任俠有名。 項籍使將兵，數窘漢王。 項籍滅，高祖購求布千金，敢有舍匿，罪三族。 布匿濮陽周氏，周氏曰：「漢求將軍急，跡且至臣家，能聽臣，臣敢進計； 即否，願先自剄。」 布許之。 乃髡鉗布，衣褐，置廣柳車中，并與其家僮數十人，之魯朱家所賣之。 朱家心知其季布也，買置田舍。 乃之雒陽見汝陰侯滕公，說曰：「季布何罪？ 臣各為其主用，職耳。 項氏臣豈可盡誅邪？ 今上始得天下，而以私怨求一人，何示不廣也！ 且以季布之賢，漢求之急如此，此不北走胡，南走越耳。 夫忌壯士以資敵國，此伍子胥所以鞭荊平之墓也。 君何不從容為上言之？」 滕公心知朱家大俠，意布匿其所，乃許諾。 侍間，果言如朱家指。 上乃赦布。 當是時，諸公皆多布能摧剛為柔，朱家亦以此名聞當世。 布召見，謝，拜郎中。
Ji Bu was a native of Chu and had earned wide fame as a knight-errant. Xiang Yu put him in command of an army, and again and again he drove the King of Han into desperate positions. After Xiang Yu fell, Gaozu posted a reward of a thousand pounds of gold for Ji Bu and announced that anyone who harbored him would face the three-clans penalty. Ji Bu took refuge with a Mr. Zhou of Puyang. Zhou told him, "The Han are hunting you relentlessly; their agents will trace you here before long. If you will listen to me, I have a plan to propose; if not, I would sooner slit my own throat first." Ji Bu agreed. They shaved his head, clamped him in the cangue, dressed him in a beggar's robe, hid him in a covered funeral wagon, and with several dozen of his household slaves drove him to the Zhu household in Lu to be sold as a slave. Zhu Jia recognized him as Ji Bu, purchased him, and lodged him on a farm. He then traveled to Luoyang to see the Marquis of Ruyin, Xiahou Ying, and argued, "What offense has Ji Bu committed? Each minister served his own lord as duty required; that was simply the nature of the office. You cannot execute every minister who once served the house of Xiang. The Son of Heaven has only just won the realm, yet he hunts one man for a private grudge—what a cramped message that sends to the world! A man of Ji Bu's caliber, hounded this fiercely, will surely flee north to the Xiongnu or south to Yue. Alienating a stalwart and strengthening a rival is how King Ping of Chu earned having his grave flogged by Wu Zixu. Why will you not find a suitable moment and put this to the throne?" Lord Teng knew Zhu Jia was a famous knight-errant and guessed Ji Bu lay concealed with him, yet he gave his word. When he next attended the emperor, he spoke exactly as Zhu Jia had urged. The emperor thereupon granted Ji Bu an amnesty. Men of standing marveled that Ji Bu could turn inflexible steel into pliant silk, and Zhu Jia's name rang through the age for it. Ji Bu was called to court, offered thanks, and was appointed a gentleman of the palace.
2
孝惠時，為中郎將。 單于嘗為書嫚呂太后，太后怒，召諸將議之。 上將軍樊噲曰：「臣願得十萬眾，橫行匈奴中。」 諸將皆阿呂太后，以噲言為然。 布曰：「樊噲可斬也。 夫以高帝兵三十餘萬，困於平城，噲時亦在其中。 今噲奈何以十萬眾橫行匈奴中，面謾！ 且秦以事胡，陳勝等起。 今瘡痍未瘳，噲又面諛，欲搖動天下。」 是時殿上皆恐，太后罷朝，遂不復議擊匈奴事。
Under Emperor Xiaohui he rose to general of the gentlemen of the palace. The Chanyu once wrote a letter grossly insulting Empress Dowager Lü, who in her fury convened the generals for counsel. Fan Kuai, the senior general, declared, "Give me one hundred thousand troops and I will ride roughshod through the heart of the Xiongnu steppe." The generals to a man curried favor with the empress dowager and agreed with Fan Kuai. Ji Bu replied, "Fan Kuai deserves execution. The High Emperor led more than three hundred thousand soldiers to Pingcheng and was surrounded there—and Fan Kuai was in that army. How, then, can the same Fan Kuai promise one hundred thousand men to rampage through the Xiongnu heartland? He is brazenly lying to your faces! Besides, the Qin court exhausted the realm on the northern frontier, and Chen Sheng and his like rose in rebellion. The realm is still lacerated and unhealed, and Fan Kuai would flatter you to your faces and unsettle the whole world again." The courtiers in the hall were stricken with fear; the empress dowager dismissed the session and never again discussed a strike against the Xiongnu.
3
布為河東守。 孝文時，人有言其賢，召欲以為御史大夫。 人又言其勇，使酒難近。 至，留邸一月，見罷。 布進曰：「臣待罪河東，陛下無故召臣，此人必有以臣欺陛下者。 今臣至，無所受事，罷去，此人必有毀臣者。 夫陛下以一人譽召臣，一人毀去臣，臣恐天下有識者聞之，有以窺陛下。」 上默然，慚曰：「河東吾股肱郡，故特召君耳。」 布之官。
Ji Bu served as governor of Hedong. Under Emperor Xiaowen, men praised his talent, and the throne summoned him intending to appoint him imperial clerk. Others spoke of his ferocity and his habit of drinking himself into a temper no one could abide. He reached the capital, was kept waiting a month in the official hostel, and was sent home without receiving any post. Ji Bu stepped forward and said, "I stand guilty of whatever fault you find in my administration of Hedong; you summoned me without cause—someone must have misled you about my record. Now I have come, been given no charge, and dismissed—someone has surely slandered me. If a single voice of praise can fetch me and a single voice of blame can send me away, discerning men everywhere will infer how shallow your judgments are." The emperor fell silent, then said with embarrassment, "Hedong is one of the sinews of the realm; I called you for that reason alone." Ji Bu returned to his duties in Hedong.
4
辯士曹丘生數招權顧金錢，事貴人趙談等，與竇長君善。 布聞，寄書諫長君曰：「吾聞曹丘生非長者，勿與通。」 及曹丘生歸，欲得書請布。 竇長君曰：「季將軍不說足下，足下無往。」 固請書，遂行。 使人先發書，布果大怒，待曹丘。 曹丘至，則揖布曰：「楚人諺曰『得黃金百，不如得季布諾』，足下何以得此聲梁楚之間哉？ 且僕與足下俱楚人，使僕游揚足下名於天下，顧不美乎？ 何足下距僕之深也！」 布乃大說。 引入，留數月，為上客，厚送之。 布名所以益聞者，曹丘揚之也。
The persuader Cao Qiusheng traded on connections and chased profit, curried favor with grandees such as Zhao Tan, and was close to Dou Zhang. Ji Bu learned of it and wrote to Dou Zhang warning him, "Cao Qiusheng is no gentleman; break off dealings with him." When Cao Qiusheng went home, he wanted a letter of introduction to Ji Bu. Dou Zhang said, "General Ji dislikes you; you had best not go." Cao pressed until Dou gave him a letter, then set out. He had a messenger deliver the letter in advance; Ji Bu was furious and waited for Cao in a rage. When Cao arrived, he bowed and said, "Chu has a saying: 'A hundred pounds of gold cannot match one promise from Ji Bu.' How did you win such renown across Liang and Chu? We are both sons of Chu; were I to trumpet your name to the world, would that not be splendid? Why do you shut me out so coldly?" Ji Bu's anger melted into delight. He brought him inside, entertained him for months as an honored guest, and sent him away with rich gifts. It was Cao Qiusheng's praise that made Ji Bu's fame resound even farther.
5
布弟季心氣蓋關中，遇人恭謹，為任俠，方數千里，士爭為死。 嘗殺人，亡吳，從爰絲匿，長事爰絲，弟畜灌夫、籍福之屬。 嘗為中司馬，中尉郅都不敢加。 少年多時時竊借其名以行。 當是時，季心以勇，布以諾，聞關中。
His younger brother Ji Xin dominated Guanzhong in reputation: courteous in person, a knight-errant whose name carried for a thousand miles, with fighting men ready to die at his call. He had once killed a man and fled to Wu, where he took refuge with Yuan Ang, served him as a patron, and treated Guan Fu and Ji Fu almost as younger brothers. He held the post of central marshal; even Commandant Zhi Du would not provoke him. Young blades constantly invoked his name to throw their weight around. In those days Guanzhong spoke of Ji Xin for valor and Ji Bu for a promise as good as gold.
6
布母弟丁公，為項羽將，逐窘高祖彭城西。 短兵接，漢王急，顧謂丁公曰：「兩賢豈相厄哉！」 丁公引兵而還。 及項王滅，丁公謁見高祖，以丁公徇軍中，曰：「丁公為項王臣不忠，使項王失天下者也。」 遂斬之，曰：「使後為人臣無傚丁公也！」
Ji Bu's uncle by marriage, Lord Ding, served Xiang Yu as a general and harried Gaozu west of Pengcheng. In close combat the King of Han was hard pressed, turned to Lord Ding, and cried, "Must two able men destroy each other?" Lord Ding withdrew his troops. After Xiang Yu fell, Lord Ding came to Gaozu, who paraded him before the host and declared, "This man was Xiang Yu's minister yet betrayed him; he is the reason Xiang Yu lost the realm." He had him beheaded, adding, "Let no future minister take Lord Ding as his model!"
7
欒布，梁人也。 彭越為家人時，嘗與布游，窮困，賣庸於齊，為酒家保。 數歲別去，而布為人所略，賣為奴於燕。 為其主家報仇，燕將臧荼舉以為都尉。 荼為燕王，布為將。 及荼反，漢擊燕，虜布。 梁王彭越聞之，乃言上，請贖布為梁大夫。 使於齊，未反，漢召彭越責以謀反，夷三族，梟首雒陽，下詔有收視者輒捕之。 布還，奏事彭越頭下，祠而哭之。 吏捕以聞。 上召布罵曰：「若與彭越反邪？ 吾禁人勿收，若獨祠而哭之，與反明矣。 趣亨之。」 方提趨湯，顧曰：「願一言而死。」 上曰：「何言？」 布曰：「方上之困彭城，敗滎陽、成皋間，項王所以不能遂西，徒以彭王居梁地，與漢合從苦楚也。 當是之時，彭王壹顧，與楚則漢破，與漢則楚破。 且垓下之會，微彭王，項氏不亡。 天下已定，彭王剖符受封，亦欲傳之萬世。 今漢壹徵兵於梁，彭王病不行，而疑以為反。 反形未見，以苛細誅之，臣恐功臣人人自危也。 今彭王已死，臣生不如死，請就亨。」 上乃釋布，拜為都尉。
Luan Bu came from Liang. While Peng Yue was still a commoner, he and Luan Bu had roamed together; destitute, they hired out as laborers in Qi and Luan tended bar in an inn. Years later they separated, and Luan Bu was seized and sold into slavery in Yan. He took revenge for his master's family, and the Yan general Zang Tu nominated him commandant. When Zang Tu became king of Yan, Luan Bu served as his general. When Zang Tu revolted, the Han forces conquered Yan and took Luan Bu prisoner. King Peng Yue of Liang petitioned the throne to ransom him and appoint him a grandee of Liang. Luan was dispatched to Qi; before he could return, the court summoned Peng Yue, condemned him for treason, wiped out three generations of his kin, exposed his head in Luoyang, and decreed immediate arrest of anyone who dared mourn or view the body. Luan Bu came back, presented his report beneath Peng Yue's severed head, offered sacrifice, and wept. Officials seized him and reported the matter. The emperor summoned Luan Bu and shouted, "Did you plot rebellion with Peng Yue? I forbade anyone to mourn him; you alone held rites and wept—that marks you plainly as his accomplice." Boil him at once." As guards dragged him toward the boiling cauldron, he turned and said, "Let me speak one sentence before I die." The emperor asked, "What is it?" Luan Bu said, "When you were pinned at Pengcheng and beaten between Xingyang and Chenggao, the only reason Xiang Yu could not drive west was that King Peng held the Liang region in alliance with Han and wore Chu down. At that moment a single shift of Peng Yue's loyalty would have shattered Han had he joined Chu, or shattered Chu had he stayed with Han. At Gaixia, without King Peng, the house of Xiang would never have been destroyed. Peace secured, King Peng took the bronze tally and hereditary fief, expecting them to pass to endless generations. Now a single levy in Liang found Peng Yue too ill to march, and you branded him a traitor for it. No overt treason had appeared; you killed him on trifling suspicions—I fear every accomplished minister now sleeps uneasy. Peng Yue is dead; I no longer care to live—send me to the cauldron." The emperor spared him and named him a commandant.
8
孝文時，為燕相，至將軍。 布稱曰：「窮困不能辱身，非人也； 富貴不能快意，非賢也。」 於是嘗有德，厚報之； 有怨，必以法滅之。 吳楚反時，以功封為鄃侯，復為燕相。 燕齊之間皆為立社，號曰欒公社。
Under Emperor Xiaowen he rose from chancellor of Yan to general. Luan Bu used to say, "To endure want yet refuse to humble the body is to fall short of being human; to enjoy wealth and rank yet gain no satisfaction is to fall short of being worthy." Those who had done him a kindness he rewarded lavishly; those who had crossed him he hunted down within the law. During the revolt of Wu and Chu he earned the title Marquis of Yu and returned as Yan's chancellor. From Yan to Qi the people built shrines to him and called them Lord Luan's altars.
9
布薨，子賁嗣侯，孝武時坐為太常犧牲不如令，國除。
Luan Bu died; his son Ben succeeded the fief, but under Emperor Wu he lost the title because, as grand libationer, the sacrificial beasts he presented failed to meet statute.
10
田叔，趙陘城人也。 其先，齊田氏也。 叔好劍，學黃老術於樂鉅公。 為人廉直，喜任俠。 游諸公，趙人舉之趙相趙午，言之趙王張敖，以為郎中。 數歲，趙王賢之，未及遷。
Tian Shu came from Xingcheng in Zhao. His forebears were the Tian lineage of Qi. He loved swordsmanship and studied Huang-Lao doctrine under Yue Ju Gong. He was honest and blunt by nature and relished the knight-errant's code. He moved in elite circles until Zhao men recommended him to Chancellor Zhao Wu, who introduced him to King Zhang Ao of Zhao, who made him a gentleman of the palace. For years the king admired him yet never advanced his rank.
11
會趙午、貫高等謀弒上，事發覺，漢下詔捕趙王及群臣反者。 趙有敢隨王，罪三族。 唯田叔、孟舒等十餘人赭衣自髡鉗，隨王至長安。 趙王敖事白，得出，廢王為宣平侯，乃進言叔等十人。 上召見，與語，漢廷臣無能出其右者。 上說，盡拜為郡守、諸侯相。 叔為漢中守十餘年。
Then Zhao Wu, Guan Gao, and others conspired to murder the emperor; when the plot surfaced, the Han court ordered the arrest of the king of Zhao and every minister implicated in treason. Zhao proclaimed that anyone who dared follow their king would face the three-clans penalty. Only Tian Shu, Meng Shu, and a dozen others shaved their heads, donned the cangue and crimson convict dress, and followed their king to Chang'an. Zhang Ao's name was cleared; he was released, stripped of his kingship and enfeoffed as Marquis of Xuanping, and then recommended Tian Shu and the other nine men to the throne. The emperor received them in audience and found that no minister at his court could match their quality. Delighted, he appointed each of them governor of a commandery or chancellor to a vassal kingdom. Tian Shu governed Hanzhong for over a decade.
12
孝文帝初立，召叔問曰：「公知天下長者乎？」 對曰：「臣何足以知之！」 上曰：「公長者，宜知之。」 叔頓首曰：「故雲中守孟舒，長者也。」 是時孟舒坐虜大入雲中免。 上曰：「先帝置孟舒雲中十餘年矣，虜常一入，孟舒不能堅守，無故士卒戰死者數百人。 長者固殺人乎？」 叔叩頭曰：「夫貫高等謀反，天子下明詔，趙有敢隨張王者罪三族，然孟舒自髡鉗，隨張王，以身死之，豈自知為雲中守哉！ 漢與楚相距，士卒罷敝，而匈奴冒頓新服北夷，來為邊寇，孟舒知士卒罷敝，不忍出言，士爭臨城死敵，如子為父，以故死者數百人，孟舒豈敺之哉！ 是乃孟舒所以為長者。」 於是上曰：「賢哉孟舒！」 復召以為雲中守。
Early in Emperor Xiaowen's reign he called Tian Shu in and asked, "Who in the empire would you call a genuinely honorable man?" Tian Shu answered, "How should a man like me presume to judge that?" The emperor said, "You are such a man yourself; you must have an opinion." Tian Shu kowtowed and named Meng Shu, the former governor of Yunzhong. Meng Shu had just been removed from office for allowing a major Xiongnu raid into Yunzhong. The emperor objected: "The late emperor kept Meng Shu at Yunzhong for years; the raiders broke in once, he failed to hold the line, and hundreds of troops died for no good reason. Is that what you call honorable conduct—to let hundreds of men die?" Tian Shu kowtowed again: "When Guan Gao plotted treason, the Son of Heaven issued an explicit decree: in Zhao, anyone who followed King Zhang Ao would face the three-clans penalty. Meng Shu still shaved his head, clamped himself in the cangue, and followed his king, ready to die with him—he could not have dreamed he would one day govern Yunzhong! Han and Chu were locked in stalemate and the troops were exhausted, while Modu had just united the northern tribes and struck the frontier. Meng Shu saw how spent his men were and would not order them out; they rushed to the ramparts and died like sons for a father, so hundreds fell—Meng Shu did not drive them to it! That is the very proof that he is a man of honor." The emperor exclaimed, "Meng Shu is a true worthy!" He recalled Meng Shu and restored him as governor of Yunzhong.
13
後數歲，叔坐法失官。 梁孝王使人殺漢議臣爰盎，景帝召叔案梁，具得其事。 還報，上曰：「梁有之乎？」 對曰：「有之。」 「事安在？」 叔曰：「上無以梁事為問也。 今梁王不伏誅，是廢漢法也； 如其伏誅，太后食不甘味，臥不安席，此憂在陛下。」 於是上大賢之，以為魯相。
Some years afterward Tian Shu lost his post over a legal infraction. King Liu Wu of Liang had the Han adviser Yuan Ang murdered; Emperor Jing sent Tian Shu to investigate the kingdom, and he uncovered the whole affair. When he reported back, the emperor asked bluntly, "Did Liang do it?" Tian Shu said, "Yes." "Where is the proof?" Tian Shu replied, "Your Majesty must not press me for the details of Liang's guilt. If the king of Liang goes unpunished, the laws of Han are meaningless; if he is executed, the empress dowager will lose her appetite and her sleep—the grief will be yours to bear." The emperor admired his candor and named him chancellor of Lu.
14
相初至官，民以王取其財物自言者百餘人。 叔取其渠率二千人笞，怒之曰：「王非汝主邪？ 何敢自言主！」 魯王聞之，大慚，發中府錢，使相償之。 相曰：「王自使人償之，不爾，是王為惡而相為善也。」
Soon after he took office, well over a hundred people petitioned that the king of Lu had seized their property. Tian Shu seized some two thousand ringleaders, had them flogged, and demanded: "Is the king of Lu not your master? How dare you bring a public complaint against your own king!" The king of Lu was mortified, opened the inner treasury, and sent funds for the chancellor to repay them. Tian Shu refused: "Let His Highness dispatch his own agents to make restitution; otherwise it would look as though the king had done wrong and the chancellor had bought virtue."
15
魯王好獵，相常從入苑中，王輒休相就館。 相常暴坐苑外，終不休，曰：「吾王暴露，獨何為舍？」 王以故不大出遊。
The king of Lu loved the chase; the chancellor usually accompanied him into the hunting park, and the king always urged him to retire to the guest quarters. Tian Shu instead sat in the open outside the enclosure and refused to leave, saying, "While my lord endures the elements, how may I seek shelter?" The king therefore curtailed his excursions.
16
數年以官卒，魯以百金祠，少子仁不受，曰：「義不傷先人名。」
He died in office a few years later. Lu offered a hundred pounds of gold for his rites, but his youngest son Tian Ren declined, declaring, "Duty forbids me to trade on my father's reputation."
17
仁以壯勇為衛將軍舍人，數從擊匈奴。 衛將軍進言仁為郎中，至二千石、丞相長史，失官。 後使刺三河，還，奏事稱意，拜為京輔都尉。 月餘，遷司直。 數歲，戾太子舉兵，仁部閉城門，令太子得亡，坐縱反者族。
Tian Ren, bold in battle, entered the household of the General of Guards as a retainer and repeatedly campaigned against the Xiongnu. The general recommended him as a gentleman of the palace; he rose to two thousand bushels and chief clerk under the chancellor, then lost his post. Later he was dispatched to inspect the Three He region; his report satisfied the throne, and he was named metropolitan commandant. Within a month he was advanced to director of correctness. When Prince Li rebelled, Tian Ren's unit sealed the gates but left the heir apparent a path to flee; he was condemned for releasing a traitor and his whole clan faced extinction.
18
贊曰：以項羽之氣，而季布以勇顯名楚，身履軍搴旗者數矣，可謂壯士。 及至困厄奴僇，苟活而不變，何也？ 彼自負其材，受辱不羞，欲有所用其未足也，故終為漢名將。 賢者誠重其死。 夫婢妾賤人，感概而自殺，非能勇也，其畫無俚之至耳。 欒布哭彭越，田叔隨張敖，赴死如歸，彼誠知所處，雖古烈士，何以加哉！
The historian's verdict: For all of Xiang Yu's towering pride, Ji Bu won renown in Chu by sheer valor, time and again charging the line and snatching enemy banners—a true fighting man. Yet when ruin reduced him to slavery, he endured humiliation rather than die—why? He trusted his abilities, bore disgrace without flinching, and waited until he could put his powers to full use—hence he ended his days as one of Han's celebrated generals. A worthy man weighs carefully how he meets his end. Maids and base folk who take offense and cut their own throats show no courage—only the shallowest desperation. Luan Bu mourned Peng Yue; Tian Shu followed Zhang Ao to the scaffold as calmly as a man going home. They knew exactly what they stood for—no hero of antiquity could surpass them.
