Volume 37: Ji Bu, Luan Bu and Tian Shu

1
季布，楚人也，為任俠有名。 項籍使將兵，數窘漢王。 項籍滅，高祖購求布千金，敢有舍匿，罪三族。 布匿濮陽周氏，周氏曰：「漢求將軍急，跡且至臣家，能聽臣，臣敢進計； 即否，願先自剄。」 布許之。 乃髡鉗布，衣褐，置廣柳車中，并與其家僮數十人，之魯朱家所賣之。 朱家心知其季布也，買置田舍。 乃之雒陽見汝陰侯滕公，說曰：「季布何罪？ 臣各為其主用，職耳。 項氏臣豈可盡誅邪？ 今上始得天下，而以私怨求一人，何示不廣也！ 且以季布之賢，漢求之急如此，此不北走胡，南走越耳。 夫忌壯士以資敵國，此伍子胥所以鞭荊平之墓也。 君何不從容為上言之？」 滕公心知朱家大俠，意布匿其所，乃許諾。 侍間，果言如朱家指。 上乃赦布。 當是時，諸公皆多布能摧剛為柔，朱家亦以此名聞當世。 布召見，謝，拜郎中。
Ji Bu came from Chu and made a name for himself as a knight-errant who took on obligations for others. Xiang Yu employed him as a general, and time and again he cornered the King of Han. After Xiang Yu fell, the High Ancestor put a bounty of a thousand pounds of gold on Ji Bu’s head, threatening execution of three collateral kin groups for anyone who sheltered him. Ji Bu took refuge with the Zhou clan in Puyang. The head of the household said: “The court is hunting you relentlessly; their agents will soon trace you here. If you will trust me, I have a plan to propose; if not, I would rather slit my own throat first.” Ji Bu agreed. They shaved his head, clamped him in a cangue, dressed him as a convict laborer, hid him in a hearselike wagon with dozens of his household retainers, and sold the whole lot to Zhu Jia of Lu. Zhu Jia recognized him at once as Ji Bu, bought him, and lodged him on an outlying farm. He then traveled to Luoyang to see the Marquis of Ruoyin, Xiahou Ying, and argued: “What crime has Ji Bu committed? Ministers each served their own master—that was simply their duty. Are we to execute every minister who once served Xiang Yu? The Son of Heaven has only just won the realm; to hunt one man out of personal spite shows how little largeness of mind he displays. Besides, a man of Ji Bu’s caliber, pressed this hard, will flee north to the Xiongnu or south to Nanyue. Hounding a stalwart into the service of a rival—that is how Wu Zixu came to flog the tomb of King Ping of Chu. Why will you not find a tactful moment to speak to the emperor on his behalf?” Xiahou Ying knew Zhu Jia was a famous knight-errant and inferred that Ji Bu lay concealed with him; he agreed to help. When a suitable moment arose at court, he repeated Zhu Jia’s argument to the throne. The emperor thereupon pardoned Ji Bu. Men of rank admired Ji Bu for turning inflexible pride into yielding survival; Zhu Jia’s reputation spread across the age for the same deed. Ji Bu was summoned, expressed his gratitude, and was appointed a gentleman of the palace.
2
孝惠時，為中郎將。 單于嘗為書嫚呂太后，太后怒，召諸將議之。 上將軍樊噲曰：「臣願得十萬眾，橫行匈奴中。」 諸將皆阿呂太后，以噲言為然。 布曰：「樊噲可斬也。 夫以高帝兵三十餘萬，困於平城，噲時亦在其中。 今噲奈何以十萬眾橫行匈奴中，面謾！ 且秦以事胡，陳勝等起。 今瘡痍未瘳，噲又面諛，欲搖動天下。」 是時殿上皆恐，太后罷朝，遂不復議擊匈奴事。
Under Emperor Hui he rose to general of the gentlemen of the palace. The Chanyu had once sent a letter that grossly insulted Empress Dowager Lü; enraged, she called the generals to council. Fan Kuai, chief among the generals, said: “Give me a hundred thousand troops and I will ride roughshod through the Xiongnu heartland.” The other generals toadied to the empress dowager and echoed Fan Kuai’s bravado. Ji Bu said: “Fan Kuai deserves execution. When the High Ancestor’s three hundred thousand–strong host was trapped at Pingcheng, Fan Kuai was in that very army. How dare he now boast that a mere hundred thousand can overrun the steppe? That is barefaced deceit before the court. Moreover, the Qin dynasty exhausted itself on the northern frontier, and Chen Sheng and his like rose in rebellion. The realm is still bleeding from those wars, and Fan Kuai compounds it with sycophantic flattery that would unsettle the whole empire.” The hall fell silent with fear; the empress dowager ended the session and never again discussed striking the Xiongnu.
3
布為河東守。 孝文時，人有言其賢，召欲以為御史大夫。 人又言其勇，使酒難近。 至，留邸一月，見罷。 布進曰：「臣待罪河東，陛下無故召臣，此人必有以臣欺陛下者。 今臣至，無所受事，罷去，此人必有毀臣者。 夫陛下以一人譽召臣，一人毀去臣，臣恐天下有識者聞之，有以窺陛下。」 上默然，慚曰：「河東吾股肱郡，故特召君耳。」 布之官。
He was appointed governor of Hedong. Under Emperor Wen, someone praised his talent, and the court summoned him intending to name him imperial clerk. Others then spoke of his rash courage and fondness for drink, calling him unapproachable. He waited a month in the official hostel and was sent home without appointment. Ji Bu stepped forward: “I was serving at my post in Hedong when you summoned me without cause—someone must have oversold me to you. I have come, been given no charge, and am dismissed—someone must have blackened my name. To elevate me on one man’s word and cast me aside on another’s will let the discerning read your intentions all too clearly.” The emperor sat in silence, then said with embarrassment: “Hedong is a pillar province of mine; I merely wished to see you.” Ji Bu returned to his duties in Hedong.
4
辯士曹丘生數招權顧金錢，事貴人趙談等，與竇長君善。 布聞，寄書諫長君曰：「吾聞曹丘生非長者，勿與通。」 及曹丘生歸，欲得書請布。 竇長君曰：「季將軍不說足下，足下無往。」 固請書，遂行。 使人先發書，布果大怒，待曹丘。 曹丘至，則揖布曰：「楚人諺曰『得黃金百，不如得季布諾』，足下何以得此聲梁楚之間哉？ 且僕與足下俱楚人，使僕游揚足下名於天下，顧不美乎？ 何足下距僕之深也！」 布乃大說。 引入，留數月，為上客，厚送之。 布名所以益聞者，曹丘揚之也。
The persuader Cao Qiusheng traded on connections for profit, curried favor with grandees like Zhao Tan, and was close to Dou Zhang, the empress’s elder brother. Ji Bu heard of it and wrote warning Dou Zhang: “Cao Qiusheng is no gentleman; keep your distance.” When Cao Qiusheng came back, he wanted a letter of introduction to Ji Bu. Dou Zhang said: “General Ji dislikes you; do not go.” Cao pressed until he got the letter and set off. He sent a messenger ahead with the missive; Ji Bu was furious and waited for him. When Cao Qiusheng arrived, he bowed and said: “Chu has a saying: ‘A hundred pounds of gold cannot match Ji Bu’s word’—how did you earn such fame from Liang to Chu? We are both sons of Chu; were I to trumpet your name across the empire, would that not be splendid? Why do you shut me out so coldly!” Ji Bu’s anger melted into delight. He brought him inside, kept him for months as an honored guest, and sent him away laden with gifts. It was Cao Qiusheng’s praise that made Ji Bu’s name resound still farther.
5
布弟季心氣蓋關中，遇人恭謹，為任俠，方數千里，士爭為死。 嘗殺人，亡吳，從爰絲匿，長事爰絲，弟畜灌夫、籍福之屬。 嘗為中司馬，中尉郅都不敢加。 少年多時時竊借其名以行。 當是時，季心以勇，布以諾，聞關中。
His younger brother Ji Xin’s reputation overshadowed Guanzhong: courteous in person, he lived the knight-errant’s code, and for a thousand miles around men vied to die for him. After killing a man he fled to Wu, hid with Yuan Ang, honored Yuan as an elder brother, and treated Guan Fu and Ji Fu as juniors. As marshal of the center guard he was untouchable—even Commandant Zhi Du would not cross him. Youths constantly invoked his name without permission to throw their weight around. In those days Guanzhong knew Ji Xin for daring and Ji Bu for keeping his promises.
6
布母弟丁公，為項羽將，逐窘高祖彭城西。 短兵接，漢王急，顧謂丁公曰：「兩賢豈相厄哉！」 丁公引兵而還。 及項王滅，丁公謁見高祖，以丁公徇軍中，曰：「丁公為項王臣不忠，使項王失天下者也。」 遂斬之，曰：「使後為人臣無傚丁公也！」
Ji Bu’s maternal uncle Ding Gong served Xiang Yu as a general and once had the High Ancestor hemmed in west of Pengcheng. In close combat the King of Han was desperate; glancing back he cried to Ding Gong: “Do two good men really have to destroy each other?” Ding Gong broke off the pursuit and withdrew. After Xiang Yu fell, Ding Gong presented himself to the founder, who had him paraded before the army, declaring: “This man was Xiang Yu’s minister yet betrayed him; he is why Xiang Yu lost the empire.” He then beheaded him, adding: “Let no future minister take Ding Gong as a model!”
7
欒布，梁人也。 彭越為家人時，嘗與布游，窮困，賣庸於齊，為酒家保。 數歲別去，而布為人所略，賣為奴於燕。 為其主家報仇，燕將臧荼舉以為都尉。 荼為燕王，布為將。 及荼反，漢擊燕，虜布。 梁王彭越聞之，乃言上，請贖布為梁大夫。 使於齊，未反，漢召彭越責以謀反，夷三族，梟首雒陽，下詔有收視者輒捕之。 布還，奏事彭越頭下，祠而哭之。 吏捕以聞。 上召布罵曰：「若與彭越反邪？ 吾禁人勿收，若獨祠而哭之，與反明矣。 趣亨之。」 方提趨湯，顧曰：「願一言而死。」 上曰：「何言？」 布曰：「方上之困彭城，敗滎陽、成皋間，項王所以不能遂西，徒以彭王居梁地，與漢合從苦楚也。 當是之時，彭王壹顧，與楚則漢破，與漢則楚破。 且垓下之會，微彭王，項氏不亡。 天下已定，彭王剖符受封，亦欲傳之萬世。 今漢壹徵兵於梁，彭王病不行，而疑以為反。 反形未見，以苛細誅之，臣恐功臣人人自危也。 今彭王已死，臣生不如死，請就亨。」 上乃釋布，拜為都尉。
Luan Bu was a native of Liang. When Peng Yue was a commoner, he and Luan Bu traveled together; broke, they hired out as laborers in Qi, with Luan tending a wineshop. Years later they separated; kidnappers sold Luan Bu into slavery in Yan. He avenged his master’s household; the Yan general Zang Tu promoted him to commandant. When Zang Tu became king of Yan, Luan Bu served as his general. When Zang Tu rebelled, Han forces attacked Yan and took Luan Bu prisoner. King Peng Yue of Liang petitioned the throne to ransom him as a Liang grandee. Luan Bu was on a mission to Qi when the court summoned Peng Yue, convicted him of treason, wiped out three kin groups, exposed Peng’s head in Luoyang, and decreed death for anyone who dared mourn or view the body. Luan Bu returned, made his report beneath Peng Yue’s severed head, offered sacrifice, and wept. Officials seized him and reported the matter. The emperor hauled him in and snarled: “Did you plot rebellion with Peng Yue? I forbade anyone to mourn him; you alone wept over him—that proves your guilt.” Boil him at once.” As guards marched him toward the boiling pot, he turned and said: “Let me speak one sentence before I die.” The emperor asked: “What is it?” Luan Bu said: “When you were trapped at Pengcheng and routed between Xingyang and Chenggao, the only reason Xiang Yu could not drive west was that Peng Yue held the Liang region in alliance with Han and wore Chu down. At that moment a single shift of Peng Yue’s loyalty would have shattered either Han or Chu. At Gaixia, without Peng Yue, Xiang Yu would never have fallen. Peace won, Peng Yue took his bronze tally and fief, expecting them to pass to endless heirs. Now a single levy in Liang found him ill and unable to march, and you branded him a traitor on suspicion alone. No rebellion showed itself, yet you killed him on trifling charges—every meritorious minister must now tremble for his life. Peng Yue is dead; for me to live would be worse than death—send me to the cauldron.” The emperor spared him and named him commandant.
8
孝文時，為燕相，至將軍。 布稱曰：「窮困不能辱身，非人也； 富貴不能快意，非賢也。」 於是嘗有德，厚報之； 有怨，必以法滅之。 吳楚反時，以功封為鄃侯，復為燕相。 燕齊之間皆為立社，號曰欒公社。
Under Emperor Wen he served as chancellor of Yan and rose to general. Luan Bu declared: “To endure want without debasing oneself is less than human; to enjoy riches without acting on one’s convictions is less than noble.” Whenever someone had done him a kindness, he repaid it many times over. If anyone had earned his hatred, he saw to it that the law brought them down. When the Wu–Chu rebellion broke out, his service earned him the marquisate of Yu and a second term as Yan’s chancellor. From Yan to Qi the people built shrines to him, calling them the altars of Lord Luan.
9
布薨，子賁嗣侯，孝武時坐為太常犧牲不如令，國除。
Luan Bu died; his son Luan Ben inherited the title, but under Emperor Wu lost the fief when, as grand master of ceremonies, he mishandled sacrificial victims.
10
田叔，趙陘城人也。 其先，齊田氏也。 叔好劍，學黃老術於樂鉅公。 為人廉直，喜任俠。 游諸公，趙人舉之趙相趙午，言之趙王張敖，以為郎中。 數歲，趙王賢之，未及遷。
Tian Shu came from Xingcheng in Zhao. His forebears were the Tian lineage of Qi. He loved swordsmanship and studied Huang–Lao doctrine under Yue Ju. Upright and incorruptible, he too lived by the knight-errant code. He moved among the powerful of Zhao; they recommended him to Chancellor Zhao Wu, who introduced him to King Zhang Ao, who appointed him a gentleman of the palace. For years the king admired him but had not yet advanced his rank.
11
會趙午、貫高等謀弒上，事發覺，漢下詔捕趙王及群臣反者。 趙有敢隨王，罪三族。 唯田叔、孟舒等十餘人赭衣自髡鉗，隨王至長安。 趙王敖事白，得出，廢王為宣平侯，乃進言叔等十人。 上召見，與語，漢廷臣無能出其右者。 上說，盡拜為郡守、諸侯相。 叔為漢中守十餘年。
Then Zhao Wu, Guan Gao, and others plotted regicide; when the plot surfaced, Han issued warrants for King Zhang Ao and every minister implicated. In Zhao anyone who dared stay with the king faced execution to the third degree of kin. Only Tian Shu, Meng Shu, and a dozen others donned the convict's russet, shaved their heads, and locked on the cangue of their own accord, then followed their king to the capital. Once King Zhang Ao was cleared, he went free and was reduced to Marquis of Xuanping; he then urged the throne to employ Tian Shu and the others. The emperor received them and talked with them; no minister at court could match their quality. Pleased, he named each of them either a commandery governor or a kingdom chancellor. Tian Shu governed Hanzhong for over a decade.
12
孝文帝初立，召叔問曰：「公知天下長者乎？」 對曰：「臣何足以知之！」 上曰：「公長者，宜知之。」 叔頓首曰：「故雲中守孟舒，長者也。」 是時孟舒坐虜大入雲中免。 上曰：「先帝置孟舒雲中十餘年矣，虜常一入，孟舒不能堅守，無故士卒戰死者數百人。 長者固殺人乎？」 叔叩頭曰：「夫貫高等謀反，天子下明詔，趙有敢隨張王者罪三族，然孟舒自髡鉗，隨張王，以身死之，豈自知為雲中守哉！ 漢與楚相距，士卒罷敝，而匈奴冒頓新服北夷，來為邊寇，孟舒知士卒罷敝，不忍出言，士爭臨城死敵，如子為父，以故死者數百人，孟舒豈敺之哉！ 是乃孟舒所以為長者。」 於是上曰：「賢哉孟舒！」 復召以為雲中守。
Early in Emperor Wen's reign he called Tian Shu in and asked: "Who in the empire still embodies old-fashioned integrity?" He answered: "I am hardly worthy to judge that." The emperor said: "You are such a man yourself; you must have an opinion." Tian Shu kowtowed and named Meng Shu, the former governor of Yunzhong. Meng Shu had just lost his post after a major Xiongnu raid on Yunzhong. The emperor objected: "The late emperor kept Meng Shu in Yunzhong for years; one raid broke through, he failed to hold the line, and hundreds of men died for nothing." Is that what you call a man of principle—to get soldiers killed?" Tian Shu kowtowed again: "When Guan Gao plotted treason, Your Majesty decreed that anyone in Zhao who stayed with King Zhang would die with three collateral lines—yet Meng Shu disfigured himself like a convict and followed his lord, ready to perish with him. He could not have foreseen he would ever govern Yunzhong." Han and Chu had bled each other white, Modu had just united the steppe, and raiders poured against the frontier. Meng Shu saw his men were spent and would not drive them; they stormed the walls and died like sons defending a father—not because he whipped them forward. That is the mark of a true elder." The emperor exclaimed: "Meng Shu is a worthy man!" Meng Shu was recalled and reappointed governor of Yunzhong.
13
後數歲，叔坐法失官。 梁孝王使人殺漢議臣爰盎，景帝召叔案梁，具得其事。 還報，上曰：「梁有之乎？」 對曰：「有之。」 「事安在？」 叔曰：「上無以梁事為問也。 今梁王不伏誅，是廢漢法也； 如其伏誅，太后食不甘味，臥不安席，此憂在陛下。」 於是上大賢之，以為魯相。
Some years later Tian Shu lost his post through a legal infraction. When King Liu Wu of Liang had Yuan Ang assassinated, Emperor Jing sent Tian Shu to probe the kingdom; he uncovered the whole plot. On his return the emperor asked point-blank: "Was Liang behind it?" Tian Shu answered: "Yes." "Where is the proof?" Tian Shu said: "You should not pursue this further." If the king of Liang goes unpunished, the law of Han is empty; if he is executed, the empress dowager will neither eat nor sleep in peace—and the burden will be yours." The emperor admired his candor and named him chancellor to the king of Lu.
14
相初至官，民以王取其財物自言者百餘人。 叔取其渠率二千人笞，怒之曰：「王非汝主邪？ 何敢自言主！」 魯王聞之，大慚，發中府錢，使相償之。 相曰：「王自使人償之，不爾，是王為惡而相為善也。」
The emperor admired his candor and named him chancellor to the king of Lu. Soon after he took office, well over a hundred commoners petitioned that the king had seized their goods. He seized some two thousand ringleaders among them, had them flogged, and thundered: "The king is your sovereign—how dare you denounce him in open court!" The king of Lu, mortified, opened his privy purse and told the chancellor to repay the people. Tian Shu refused: "Your Highness must make the restitution yourself; otherwise the people will praise your minister and blame their prince."
15
魯王好獵，相常從入苑中，王輒休相就館。 相常暴坐苑外，終不休，曰：「吾王暴露，獨何為舍？」 王以故不大出遊。
The king of Lu loved the chase; Tian Shu always rode into the preserve with him, but the king kept sending him back to the guesthouse to rest. Tian Shu instead sat in the open beyond the wall, refusing to leave, saying: "If my lord endures the weather, how may I hide indoors?" The king therefore hunted far less often.
16
數年以官卒，魯以百金祠，少子仁不受，曰：「義不傷先人名。」
He died in harness a few years later. Lu offered his family a hundred pounds of gold for funeral rites; his youngest son Tian Ren declined, saying: "Accepting would tarnish my father's reputation."
17
仁以壯勇為衛將軍舍人，數從擊匈奴。 衛將軍進言仁為郎中，至二千石、丞相長史，失官。 後使刺三河，還，奏事稱意，拜為京輔都尉。 月餘，遷司直。 數歲，戾太子舉兵，仁部閉城門，令太子得亡，坐縱反者族。
Tian Ren, bold and hardy, became a client of General-in-chief Wei Qing and repeatedly campaigned against the Xiongnu. Wei Qing recommended him as a gentleman of the palace; he rose to two thousand piculs and chief clerk under the chancellor before losing his post. Later, as an inspector of the Three He commanderies, he filed reports that satisfied the emperor and was named capital-area commandant. Within a month he advanced to metropolitan clerk. Years afterward, when Prince Li mutiny broke out, Tian Ren's unit barred the gates but allowed the heir to flee; he was convicted of abetting rebellion and his whole clan was executed.
18
贊曰：以項羽之氣，而季布以勇顯名楚，身履軍搴旗者數矣，可謂壯士。 及至困厄奴僇，苟活而不變，何也？ 彼自負其材，受辱不羞，欲有所用其未足也，故終為漢名將。 賢者誠重其死。 夫婢妾賤人，感概而自殺，非能勇也，其畫無俚之至耳。 欒布哭彭越，田叔隨張敖，赴死如歸，彼誠知所處，雖古烈士，何以加哉！
The historian's judgment: Even under Xiang Yu's towering pride, Ji Bu made a name in Chu for valor, time and again seizing standards in the melee—a true fighting man. Why, then, did he endure the shame of chains and servitude rather than die? They trusted their gifts: humiliation meant little if they could yet serve; only thus did they become celebrated Han generals. Men of worth take death seriously. Bondmaids who stab themselves in a fit of pique show no bravery—only the pettiest spite. Luan Bu mourning Peng Yue, Tian Shu cleaving to Zhang Ao—they marched toward execution as toward a feast, knowing exactly where they stood; the ancient paragons of fidelity could do no more.
