1
季布，楚人也，為任俠有名。 項籍使將兵，數窘漢王。 項籍滅，高祖購求布千金，敢有舍匿，罪三族。 布匿濮陽周氏，周氏曰：「漢求將軍急，跡且至臣家，能聽臣，臣敢進計； 即否，願先自剄。」 布許之。 乃髡鉗布，衣褐，置廣柳車中，并與其家僮數十人，之魯朱家所賣之。 朱家心知其季布也，買置田舍。 乃之雒陽見汝陰侯滕公，說曰：「季布何罪？ 臣各為其主用，職耳。 項氏臣豈可盡誅邪？ 今上始得天下，而以私怨求一人，何示不廣也！ 且以季布之賢，漢求之急如此，此不北走胡，南走越耳。 夫忌壯士以資敵國，此伍子胥所以鞭荊平之墓也。 君何不從容為上言之？」 滕公心知朱家大俠，意布匿其所，乃許諾。 侍間，果言如朱家指。 上乃赦布。 當是時，諸公皆多布能摧剛為柔，朱家亦以此名聞當世。 布召見，謝，拜郎中。
Ji Bu was a native of Chu and was widely known as a knight-errant. Xiang Yu sent him to lead troops, and time and again he drove the King of Han into desperate straits. After Xiang Yu fell, Gaozu offered a thousand catties of gold for Ji Bu's capture, and anyone who dared shelter or hide him would be punished with extermination of three generations of his kin. Ji Bu took refuge with the Zhou family of Puyang. The head of the family said, "The Han court is hunting you with great urgency, and their agents will soon trace you here. If you will listen to me, I venture to propose a plan;" If not, I would rather cut my own throat first. Ji Bu agreed. They shaved Ji Bu's head, fitted him with an iron collar, dressed him in a laborer's garb, and stowed him in a covered funeral cart along with several dozen household retainers sold as bondservants, then took the party to Lu and sold them to Zhu Jia. Zhu Jia knew at heart that the man was Ji Bu; he purchased him and settled him on a farm. He then traveled to Luoyang to see the Marquis of Ru-yin, Lord Teng (Xiahou Ying), and urged him: "What crime has Ji Bu committed?" Each minister served the lord he owed allegiance to; that was simply his duty. Surely you cannot put every minister who served the Xiangs to the sword? The Son of Heaven has only just won the empire; to hunt one man out of private spite shows how narrow his magnanimity would appear! Besides, a man of Ji Bu's caliber, pressed this hard by the Han, will flee north to the Hu or south to Yue—that is all there is to it. To drive a brave man into the arms of a rival power out of jealousy is the very reason Wu Zixu lashed the grave of King Ping of Chu. Why do you not find a suitable moment and put this to the sovereign for him? Lord Teng knew Zhu Jia for a great knight-errant and guessed that Ji Bu lay hidden with him; he gave his word. When an opportunity arose, he spoke to the emperor exactly as Zhu Jia had directed. The emperor thereupon pardoned Ji Bu. At that time eminent men alike admired Ji Bu for turning inflexible strength into yielding survival, and Zhu Jia's reputation spread through the age for the same deed. Ji Bu was summoned into audience, expressed his gratitude, and was appointed Gentleman of the Palace.
2
孝惠時，為中郎將。 單于嘗為書嫚呂太后，太后怒，召諸將議之。 上將軍樊噲曰：「臣願得十萬眾，橫行匈奴中。」 諸將皆阿呂太后，以噲言為然。 布曰：「樊噲可斬也。 夫以高帝兵三十餘萬，困於平城，噲時亦在其中。 今噲奈何以十萬眾橫行匈奴中，面謾！ 且秦以事胡，陳勝等起。 今瘡痍未瘳，噲又面諛，欲搖動天下。」 是時殿上皆恐，太后罷朝，遂不復議擊匈奴事。
Under Emperor Xiaohui he served as General of the Gentlemen of the Palace. The Chanyu had once sent a letter that insulted Empress Dowager Lü; enraged, she called in the generals to discuss a response. Senior General Fan Kuai declared, "I ask for a hundred thousand men to sweep through the heart of the Xiongnu domain." The generals all curried favor with Empress Dowager Lü and agreed that Fan Kuai was right. Ji Bu said, "Fan Kuai deserves execution." Gaodi led more than three hundred thousand men and was still trapped at Pingcheng, and Fan Kuai was in that army. How dare Fan Kuai now claim he can ride roughshod over the Xiongnu with a mere hundred thousand—he insults us to our faces! Moreover, when Qin exhausted itself on the northern tribes, Chen Sheng and his like rose in rebellion. The wounds of war are not yet healed, and Fan Kuai flatters you to your face in a bid to unsettle the whole realm. A chill fell over everyone in the hall; the empress dowager ended the audience, and the court never again debated attacking the Xiongnu.
3
布為河東守。 孝文時，人有言其賢，召欲以為御史大夫。 人又言其勇，使酒難近。 至，留邸一月，見罷。 布進曰：「臣待罪河東，陛下無故召臣，此人必有以臣欺陛下者。 今臣至，無所受事，罷去，此人必有毀臣者。 夫陛下以一人譽召臣，一人毀去臣，臣恐天下有識者聞之，有以窺陛下。」 上默然，慚曰：「河東吾股肱郡，故特召君耳。」 布之官。
Ji Bu served as governor of Hedong. Under Emperor Xiaowen, someone praised his abilities, and the emperor summoned him intending to appoint him Grandee Secretary. Others spoke of his courage but added that he was unruly when drunk and difficult to work with. When he arrived, he was kept at the official lodging for a month and then dismissed without appointment. Ji Bu stepped forward and said, "I stand awaiting judgment in Hedong; Your Majesty summoned me without cause—someone must have misrepresented me to you." Now that I have come and been given no assignment but sent away, someone must have slandered me. If a single man's praise can bring me to court and a single man's blame can send me away, I fear that discerning men everywhere will draw their own conclusions about Your Majesty's judgment. The emperor was silent, then said with embarrassment, "Hedong is a pillar of the realm; that is why I called you here in particular." Ji Bu returned to his post.
4
辯士曹丘生數招權顧金錢，事貴人趙談等，與竇長君善。 布聞，寄書諫長君曰：「吾聞曹丘生非長者，勿與通。」 及曹丘生歸，欲得書請布。 竇長君曰：「季將軍不說足下，足下無往。」 固請書，遂行。 使人先發書，布果大怒，待曹丘。 曹丘至，則揖布曰：「楚人諺曰『得黃金百，不如得季布諾』，足下何以得此聲梁楚之間哉？ 且僕與足下俱楚人，使僕游揚足下名於天下，顧不美乎？ 何足下距僕之深也！」 布乃大說。 引入，留數月，為上客，厚送之。 布名所以益聞者，曹丘揚之也。
The rhetorician Cao Qiusheng often traded on influence for money, cultivated men such as the favorite Zhao Tan, and was on close terms with Dou Changjun. When Ji Bu heard of it, he sent Dou Changjun a letter of warning: "I have heard that Cao Qiusheng is no man of honor; have nothing to do with him." When Cao Qiusheng came home, he wanted a letter of introduction so he could call on Ji Bu. Dou Changjun said, "General Ji dislikes you; you had better not go." Cao pressed for the letter until he got it, then set out. He sent someone ahead with the letter; Ji Bu was furious and waited for Cao Qiusheng in a rage. When Cao Qiusheng arrived, he bowed to Ji Bu and said, "The people of Chu have a saying: 'A hundred catties of gold are not worth Ji Bu's word.' How did you win such a name across Liang and Chu?" Moreover, you and I are both men of Chu; if I spread your reputation through the empire, would that not be splendid? Why do you keep me at such a distance! Ji Bu was greatly pleased. He brought him inside, kept him for several months as an honored guest, and sent him away with rich gifts. The reason Ji Bu's fame grew still wider was that Cao Qiusheng proclaimed it.
5
布弟季心氣蓋關中，遇人恭謹，為任俠，方數千里，士爭為死。 嘗殺人，亡吳，從爰絲匿，長事爰絲，弟畜灌夫、籍福之屬。 嘗為中司馬，中尉郅都不敢加。 少年多時時竊借其名以行。 當是時，季心以勇，布以諾，聞關中。
Ji Bu's younger brother Ji Xin's daring overshadowed the Guanzhong region; though courteous and restrained in personal dealings, as a knight-errant his reputation spread for thousands of li, and fighting men vied to die for him. He once killed a man and fled to Wu, where he took refuge with Yuan Ang and served him with the deference due an elder, while treating Guan Fu, Ji Fu, and their circle as younger companions. He had served as a marshal in the central command; even Commandant Zhi Du did not dare cross him. Young men often quietly traded on his name to get their way. In those days Ji Xin was famed in Guanzhong for his bravery and Ji Bu for keeping his word.
6
布母弟丁公，為項羽將，逐窘高祖彭城西。 短兵接，漢王急，顧謂丁公曰：「兩賢豈相厄哉！」 丁公引兵而還。 及項王滅，丁公謁見高祖，以丁公徇軍中，曰：「丁公為項王臣不忠，使項王失天下者也。」 遂斬之，曰：「使後為人臣無傚丁公也！」
Ji Bu's maternal uncle Lord Ding was a general under Xiang Yu and harried Gaozu into a tight corner west of Pengcheng. At close quarters the King of Han was desperate; turning to Lord Ding he cried, "Surely two able men need not ruin each other!" Lord Ding withdrew his troops and turned back. After King Xiang fell, Lord Ding came to pay his respects to Gaozu; Gaozu had him paraded before the army and declared, "Lord Ding was a disloyal minister to King Xiang and was the man who cost him the empire." He had him executed, saying, "Let no minister hereafter take Lord Ding as his model!"
7
欒布，梁人也。 彭越為家人時，嘗與布游，窮困，賣庸於齊，為酒家保。 數歲別去，而布為人所略，賣為奴於燕。 為其主家報仇，燕將臧荼舉以為都尉。 荼為燕王，布為將。 及荼反，漢擊燕，虜布。 梁王彭越聞之，乃言上，請贖布為梁大夫。 使於齊，未反，漢召彭越責以謀反，夷三族，梟首雒陽，下詔有收視者輒捕之。 布還，奏事彭越頭下，祠而哭之。 吏捕以聞。 上召布罵曰：「若與彭越反邪？ 吾禁人勿收，若獨祠而哭之，與反明矣。 趣亨之。」 方提趨湯，顧曰：「願一言而死。」 上曰：「何言？」 布曰：「方上之困彭城，敗滎陽、成皋間，項王所以不能遂西，徒以彭王居梁地，與漢合從苦楚也。 當是之時，彭王壹顧，與楚則漢破，與漢則楚破。 且垓下之會，微彭王，項氏不亡。 天下已定，彭王剖符受封，亦欲傳之萬世。 今漢壹徵兵於梁，彭王病不行，而疑以為反。 反形未見，以苛細誅之，臣恐功臣人人自危也。 今彭王已死，臣生不如死，請就亨。」 上乃釋布，拜為都尉。
Luan Bu was a native of Liang. When Peng Yue was still a commoner, he had roamed with Luan Bu; in destitution he hired out his labor in Qi and worked as a bondservant in a tavern. After some years they parted, and Luan Bu was kidnapped and sold into slavery in Yan. He avenged a wrong for his master's family, whereupon the Yan general Zang Tu promoted him to commandant. When Zang Tu became King of Yan, Luan Bu served as his general. When Zang Tu rebelled, the Han attacked Yan and took Luan Bu prisoner. King Peng Yue of Liang heard of it and petitioned the emperor to ransom Luan Bu and appoint him a grandee of Liang. Luan Bu was on a mission to Qi and had not returned when the Han summoned Peng Yue, charged him with plotting rebellion, wiped out three clans of his kin, exposed his head in Luoyang, and issued an edict that anyone who dared mourn or bury him would be arrested at once. When Luan Bu returned, he presented his report beneath Peng Yue's severed head, then offered sacrifice and wept for him. The clerks seized him and reported the matter. The emperor summoned Luan Bu and shouted, "Were you in rebellion with Peng Yue?" I forbade anyone to claim his body; you alone mourned him with sacrifice and tears—your guilt is as plain as if you had risen in arms. Take him away and boil him alive. As they were dragging him toward the cauldron, he looked back and said, "Let me speak one word before I die." The emperor said, "What is it?" Luan Bu said, "When Your Majesty was pinned down at Pengcheng and driven from Xingyang to Chenggao, the reason King Xiang could not push west was solely that the King of Peng held Liang and allied with Han to exhaust Chu." In those days a single shift of the King of Peng's weight—if he had thrown in with Chu, Han would have shattered; if with Han, Chu would have shattered. Moreover, at the battle of Gaixia, had it not been for the King of Peng, the house of Xiang would not have fallen. Once the realm was settled, the King of Peng received his tally and fief and hoped to hand them down for ages. Now the Han issued one levy in Liang; the king was ill and could not march, and on that slender ground he was suspected of treason. No sign of rebellion had appeared; to put him to death on petty charges makes me fear that every meritorious minister will begin to doubt his own safety. The King of Peng is already dead; I would rather die than live—send me to the cauldron. The emperor released him and appointed him commandant.
8
孝文時，為燕相，至將軍。 布稱曰：「窮困不能辱身，非人也； 富貴不能快意，非賢也。」 於是嘗有德，厚報之； 有怨，必以法滅之。 吳楚反時，以功封為鄃侯，復為燕相。 燕齊之間皆為立社，號曰欒公社。
Under Emperor Xiaowen he served as chancellor of Yan and rose to general. Luan Bu used to say, "To be down and out yet refuse to demean oneself is not to be human;" to be rich and honored yet unable to follow one's conscience is not to be worthy. Accordingly, those who had once done him a kindness he repaid handsomely; those who had wronged him he was sure to destroy within the law. During the rebellion of Wu and Chu he was enfeoffed as Marquis of Yu for his service and again became chancellor of Yan. Between Yan and Qi the people founded shrines to him and called them the altars of Duke Luan.
9
布薨，子賁嗣侯，孝武時坐為太常犧牲不如令，國除。
When Luan Bu died, his son Ben inherited the marquisate; under Emperor Wu he was convicted because sacrificial animals under his charge as Grand Master of Ceremonies failed to meet regulations, and the fief was abolished.
10
田叔，趙陘城人也。 其先，齊田氏也。 叔好劍，學黃老術於樂鉅公。 為人廉直，喜任俠。 游諸公，趙人舉之趙相趙午，言之趙王張敖，以為郎中。 數歲，趙王賢之，未及遷。
Tian Shu was a native of Xingcheng in Zhao. His ancestors were the Tian clan of Qi. Tian Shu loved the sword and studied the teachings of Huangdi and Laozi under Master Yue Ju. He was incorruptible and upright by nature and delighted in knight-errantry. He moved among the great houses until men of Zhao recommended him to the Zhao chancellor Zhao Wu, who spoke of him to King Zhang Ao of Zhao, who appointed him Gentleman of the Palace. For several years the king of Zhao admired him but had not yet promoted him.
11
會趙午、貫高等謀弒上，事發覺，漢下詔捕趙王及群臣反者。 趙有敢隨王，罪三族。 唯田叔、孟舒等十餘人赭衣自髡鉗，隨王至長安。 趙王敖事白，得出，廢王為宣平侯，乃進言叔等十人。 上召見，與語，漢廷臣無能出其右者。 上說，盡拜為郡守、諸侯相。 叔為漢中守十餘年。
It happened that Zhao Wu, Guan Gao, and others plotted to murder the emperor; when the plot was uncovered, the Han issued an edict to seize the king of Zhao and all ministers involved in the conspiracy. In Zhao, anyone who dared follow the king would suffer extermination of three clans. Only Tian Shu, Meng Shu, and a dozen others shaved their heads, donned the crimson convict garb and iron collar, and followed their king to Chang'an. When King Zhang Ao's case was cleared, he was released, stripped of his kingship, and enfeoffed as Marquis of Xuanping; he then recommended Tian Shu and the other ten men. The emperor received them in audience and spoke with them, and no minister at the Han court proved their superior. Pleased, the emperor appointed them all governors or chancellors to feudal kings. Tian Shu served as governor of Hanzhong for more than ten years.
12
孝文帝初立，召叔問曰：「公知天下長者乎？」 對曰：「臣何足以知之！」 上曰：「公長者，宜知之。」 叔頓首曰：「故雲中守孟舒，長者也。」 是時孟舒坐虜大入雲中免。 上曰：「先帝置孟舒雲中十餘年矣，虜常一入，孟舒不能堅守，無故士卒戰死者數百人。 長者固殺人乎？」 叔叩頭曰：「夫貫高等謀反，天子下明詔，趙有敢隨張王者罪三族，然孟舒自髡鉗，隨張王，以身死之，豈自知為雲中守哉！ 漢與楚相距，士卒罷敝，而匈奴冒頓新服北夷，來為邊寇，孟舒知士卒罷敝，不忍出言，士爭臨城死敵，如子為父，以故死者數百人，孟舒豈敺之哉！ 是乃孟舒所以為長者。」 於是上曰：「賢哉孟舒！」 復召以為雲中守。
Soon after Emperor Xiaowen came to the throne, he summoned Tian Shu and asked, "Do you know who in the realm are true men of honor?" He replied, "How could your servant be fit to judge such men!" The emperor said, "You are a man of honor yourself; you ought to know." Tian Shu kowtowed and said, "The former governor of Yunzhong, Meng Shu, is such a man." At that time Meng Shu had been removed from office because the barbarians had raided Yunzhong in force. The emperor said, "The late emperor kept Meng Shu at Yunzhong for more than ten years, yet when the barbarians broke in he could not hold the line, and for no good reason several hundred soldiers died in the fighting." Is that how a man of honor kills his own men? Tian Shu kowtowed and said, "When Guan Gao and the others plotted treason, the Son of Heaven issued a clear edict that anyone in Zhao who dared follow King Zhang would suffer extermination of three clans; yet Meng Shu shaved his head and put on the collar of a convict and followed King Zhang, ready to die for him—how could he have known he would one day be governor of Yunzhong!" Han and Chu were locked in stalemate and the troops were worn out, while Modu of the Xiongnu had just brought the northern tribes to heel and turned them against our borders. Meng Shu saw how spent his men were and could not bring himself to give the harsh order; the soldiers vied to man the walls and die against the foe like sons for a father, and so several hundred perished—surely Meng Shu did not drive them to it! That is precisely why Meng Shu is a true man of honor. The emperor exclaimed, "Meng Shu is a worthy man indeed!" He summoned him again and appointed him governor of Yunzhong.
13
後數歲，叔坐法失官。 梁孝王使人殺漢議臣爰盎，景帝召叔案梁，具得其事。 還報，上曰：「梁有之乎？」 對曰：「有之。」 「事安在？」 叔曰：「上無以梁事為問也。 今梁王不伏誅，是廢漢法也； 如其伏誅，太后食不甘味，臥不安席，此憂在陛下。」 於是上大賢之，以為魯相。
Some years later Tian Shu lost his post for a legal infraction. King Xiao of Liang had had the Han counselor Yuan Ang murdered; Emperor Jing summoned Tian Shu to investigate the kingdom of Liang, and he uncovered the whole affair. When he returned and reported, the emperor asked, "Did Liang do it?" He answered, "It did." "Where is the proof?" Tian Shu said, "Your Majesty should not press the matter of Liang." If the king of Liang is not made to answer with his life, the laws of Han are set aside; if he is executed, the empress dowager will lose her appetite and sleep—the burden of that grief will fall on Your Majesty. The emperor admired him greatly and appointed him chancellor to the king of Lu.
14
相初至官，民以王取其財物自言者百餘人。 叔取其渠率二千人笞，怒之曰：「王非汝主邪？ 何敢自言主！」 魯王聞之，大慚，發中府錢，使相償之。 相曰：「王自使人償之，不爾，是王為惡而相為善也。」
When the chancellor first took up his post, more than a hundred commoners came forward claiming that the king had seized their property. Tian Shu had the ringleaders among them—some two thousand men—flogged and rebuked them: "Is the king not your sovereign?" You would dare denounce your sovereign in public! When the king of Lu heard of this, he was deeply ashamed; he drew funds from the inner treasury and told the chancellor to make restitution. The chancellor replied, "Let the king send his own men to repay them; otherwise it will look as though the king did wrong and the chancellor played the benefactor."
15
魯王好獵，相常從入苑中，王輒休相就館。 相常暴坐苑外，終不休，曰：「吾王暴露，獨何為舍？」 王以故不大出遊。
The king of Lu loved the chase, and the chancellor often accompanied him into the hunting park; the king would send the chancellor off to the lodge to rest. The chancellor would sit in the open outside the park and refuse to go in, saying, "My king endures the elements; why should I alone take shelter?" For this reason the king seldom went out on long hunts.
16
數年以官卒，魯以百金祠，少子仁不受，曰：「義不傷先人名。」
Some years later he died in office; Lu offered a hundred catties of gold for his rites, but his youngest son Ren refused it, saying, "Duty does not permit me to tarnish my father's name."
17
仁以壯勇為衛將軍舍人，數從擊匈奴。 衛將軍進言仁為郎中，至二千石、丞相長史，失官。 後使刺三河，還，奏事稱意，拜為京輔都尉。 月餘，遷司直。 數歲，戾太子舉兵，仁部閉城門，令太子得亡，坐縱反者族。
Ren, known for his vigor and courage, became a retainer of the General of the Guards and repeatedly accompanied him on campaigns against the Xiongnu. The General of the Guards recommended him for Gentleman of the Palace; he rose to two-thousand-dan rank and chief clerk to the chancellor, then lost his post. Later he was sent to inspect the Three He commanderies; on his return his report pleased the emperor, and he was appointed Metropolitan Commandant. A little over a month later he was promoted to Director of Integrity. Some years later, when Crown Prince Ju rose in revolt, Tian Ren's men manipulated the city gates so that the heir could escape; he was convicted of abetting rebels and his entire clan was put to death.
18
贊曰：以項羽之氣，而季布以勇顯名楚，身履軍搴旗者數矣，可謂壯士。 及至困厄奴僇，苟活而不變，何也？ 彼自負其材，受辱不羞，欲有所用其未足也，故終為漢名將。 賢者誠重其死。 夫婢妾賤人，感概而自殺，非能勇也，其畫無俚之至耳。 欒布哭彭越，田叔隨張敖，赴死如歸，彼誠知所處，雖古烈士，何以加哉！
The historian writes: In an age dominated by Xiang Yu's fire, Ji Bu won renown in Chu for his courage; again and again he marched into battle and tore the enemy's banners from their poles—here was a true fighting man. Yet when reduced to bondage and humiliation he clung to life without wavering—why? He trusted in his own abilities and did not count disgrace a disgrace, for he still hoped to put his powers to use; that is why he ended his career as a celebrated general of Han. The wise truly weigh the value of their lives. Maids, concubines, and other mean folk who take umbrage and cut their own throats are not displaying courage; their judgment is simply as shallow as can be. Luan Bu wept for Peng Yue; Tian Shu followed Zhang Ao to the grave as cheerfully as if going home—they knew exactly where they stood; even the sternest heroes of old could scarcely surpass them!
