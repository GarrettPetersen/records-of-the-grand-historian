Their ministers and advisors cannot be specifically named, but the various philosophical schools discuss them to some extent. Though they don't follow Confucius's evaluations, these accounts are still preserved in the writings, serving to praise virtue and expose vice, to admonish and warn future generations. Therefore, I have gathered them broadly. Confucius said: 'As for sagehood and benevolence, how would I dare claim such things?' He also said: 'What is the point of mere benevolence? Sagehood must be the ultimate goal!' 'If someone lacks knowledge, how can they be benevolent?' 'A sage I cannot hope to see; to see a gentleman would be sufficient.' 'It is only in the depth of winter that one knows the pine and cypress are the last to lose their leaves.'
