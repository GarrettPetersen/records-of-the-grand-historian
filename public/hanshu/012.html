Appraisal: Emperor Ai, from serving as a feudal king and filling the crown prince's palace, possessed broadly perceptive literary works; from youth he had an excellent reputation. He observed that in Emperor Cheng's era, hereditary emoluments had departed from the imperial house and authority had shifted outward. Therefore, facing the court, he executed and censured ministers, desiring to strengthen the ruler's authority and follow Emperors Wu and Xuan.
