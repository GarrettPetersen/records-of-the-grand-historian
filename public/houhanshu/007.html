In the eighth month, Emperor Shang passed away; the Empress Dowager, together with her brother General Deng Zhi, determined the succession plan in the imperial residence. That night, she sent Deng Zhi with the imperial seal to welcome the emperor using the king's blue-canopied carriage; he purified himself in the hall. The Empress Dowager presided in the Chongde Hall; all officials wore auspicious robes and the assembled ministers stood in attendance as they led the emperor to bow and be appointed Marquis of Chang'an. The Empress Dowager issued an edict stating: 'The late Emperor's sage virtue was pure and flourishing, yet he abandoned the realm too early.' I serve as regent for the emperor, gazing constantly at the sun and moon, hoping for his accomplishments. Who could have expected this sudden upheaval, with his heavenly lifespan not fulfilled? The sorrow and pain break my heart. I consider that King Pingyuan has long suffered from chronic illness. Mindful of the importance of the ancestral temples and thinking of the continuity of succession, only the Marquis of Chang'an Hu possesses a loyal and filial nature, is careful and cautious, comprehends the Poetry and Discourses, is devoted to study and delights in antiquity, and is benevolent and gracious toward the people. Already thirteen years old, he has the aspirations of an adult. In terms of kinship virtue continuing the succession, none is more suitable than Hu. As the Rituals say, 'A brother's son is like one's own son'; According to the Spring and Autumn Annals, one who succeeds another becomes his son; he does not decline the grandfather's command because of the father's command. Let Hu become the heir of Emperor Xiaohe, to serve and support the ancestors; present this according to ritual and ceremony.'' Moreover, an investiture edict was composed stating: 'In the Yanping first year, autumn, eighth month, on the Guiyou day, the Empress Dowager said: We consult the Marquis of Chang'an Hu: Emperor Xiaohe's excellent virtue towers high and shines upon the four seas;' the recently departed Emperor did not fulfill his heavenly lifespan. I consider that the Marquis is Emperor Zhang's legitimate grandson by direct descent; he is modest and respectful, compassionate and obedient, diligent even in youth; he is suitable to serve the suburban altars and ancestral temples, and inherit the great enterprise. Now the Marquis shall succeed as Emperor Xiaohe's heir. Let him carefully rule the Han state, faithfully holding to the mean. 'When one person has good fortune, the ten thousand people rely upon it.' Emperor, let him strive for it!'' After the edict was read, the Grand Marshal presented the seal and tassels; he ascended the imperial throne at age thirteen. The Empress Dowager still held court.
