Zhou Houji was named Qi. His mother was a woman of the Youtai clan, named Jiang Yuan. Jiang Yuan was the primary consort of Emperor Ku. Jiang Yuan went out into the wilderness and saw giant footprints. Her heart was filled with joy; she wished to step on them, and when she did, her body moved as if she were pregnant. She carried the child to term and gave birth to a son, but considered it an ill omen and abandoned him in a narrow alley. Horses and oxen passing by all avoided stepping on him. She moved him to the forest, but by chance met many woodcutters who moved him away. She abandoned him in a ditch on the ice, but flying birds covered and protected him with their wings. Jiang Yuan considered this divine and took him in to raise and care for him. Initially she wanted to abandon him, so she named him Qi.
