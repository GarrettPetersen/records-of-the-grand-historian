The Zhou ancestor Houji was named Qi. His mother was a woman of the Youtai clan named Jiang Yuan. Jiang Yuan was the primary consort of Emperor Ku. Jiang Yuan went out into the wilderness and saw giant footprints. Her heart was filled with delight; she wished to step on them, and when she did, her body moved as if she were with child. She carried the child to term and gave birth to a son, but considered it an ill omen and abandoned him in a narrow alley. Horses and oxen passing by all avoided stepping on him. She moved him to the forest, but by chance encountered many woodcutters who moved him elsewhere. She abandoned him in a ditch on the ice, but flying birds covered and protected him with their wings. Jiang Yuan considered this a divine sign and took him in to raise. Since she had initially wanted to abandon him, she named him Qi.

Even as a child, Qi had the aspirations of a giant. In his play, he loved planting hemp and soybeans; they grew beautifully. When he became an adult, he loved farming. Bu Ku lost his position and fled among the Rong and Di barbarians. Bu Ku died, and his son Ju succeeded him. Ju died, and his son Gong Liu succeeded him.
