Zhou's Houji was named Qi (meaning "Abandoned"). His mother was a woman of the Youtai clan, named Jiang Yuan. Jiang Yuan was the principal consort of Emperor Ku. Jiang Yuan went out to the fields and saw a giant's footprint; her heart was delighted, and she wanted to step in it. When she stepped in it, her body stirred as if she were pregnant. After the full term she gave birth to a son; considering this inauspicious, she abandoned him in a narrow alley, but horses and oxen that passed all avoided him and did not trample him. She moved and placed him in the forest, but it happened that many people were in the mountains and forests, so she moved him again. Then she abandoned him on the ice in a ditch, but flying birds covered and protected him with their wings. Jiang Yuan considered this divine intervention, and thereupon took him in and raised him. Because she had initially wanted to abandon him, she named him Qi ("Abandoned").
