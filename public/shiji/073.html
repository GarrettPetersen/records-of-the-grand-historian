Bai Qi was a native of Mei. He was skilled in warfare and served King Zhao of Qin. In the thirteenth year of King Zhao's reign, Bai Qi became left senior minister and led troops to attack Han's Xincheng. That year, the Marquis of Rang served as prime minister of Qin and appointed Ren Bi as governor of Hanzhong. The next year, Bai Qi became left minister of the interior and attacked Han and Wei at Yique. He beheaded 240,000 men, captured their general Gongsun Xi, and took five cities. Qi was promoted to state defender. He crossed the Yellow River and took Han's Anyi and eastward to Ganhe. The next year, Bai Qi became grand master craftsman. He attacked Wei, conquered it, and took sixty-one cities of varying sizes. The next year, Qi and guest minister Cuo attacked Yuan City and conquered it. Five years later, Bai Qi attacked Zhao and conquered Guanglang City. Seven years later, Bai Qi attacked Chu and conquered Yan and Dengâ€”five cities in total. The next year, he attacked Chu again, conquered Ying, burned Yiling, and advanced east to Jingling. The King of Chu fled Ying and moved east to Chen. Qin made Ying into Nanhun Commandery. Bai Qi was promoted to Lord Wu'an. Lord Wu'an then conquered Chu and established the commanderies of Wu and Qianzhong. In the thirty-fourth year of King Zhao, Bai Qi attacked Wei, conquered Huayang, routed Mang Mao, captured three Jin generals, and beheaded 130,000 men. He fought with Zhao general Jia Yan and drowned 20,000 of his troops in the river. In the forty-third year of King Zhao, Bai Qi attacked Han's Xingcheng, conquered five cities, and beheaded 50,000 men. In the forty-fourth year, Bai Qi attacked the Nanyang-Taihang road and cut it off.
