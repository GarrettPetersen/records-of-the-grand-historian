Wei Bao was a prince of the former state of Wei. His elder brother Wei Jiu had been enfeoffed as Lord of Ningling during the former Wei dynasty. When Qin destroyed Wei, they demoted Wei Jiu to commoner status. When Chen Sheng rose up to become king, Wei Jiu went to join him. The King of Chen sent the Wei native Zhou Shi to pacify the Wei territories. After the Wei lands had submitted, they wanted to jointly establish Zhou Shi as King of Wei. Zhou Shi said: 'When the world is in chaos, loyal ministers make themselves known. Now the whole world is rebelling against Qin together. For righteousness, we must establish a descendant of the Wei royal family to be permissible.' Qi and Zhao sent chariots, each state providing fifty, and they wanted to jointly establish Zhou Shi as King of Wei. Zhou Shi declined the position and went to Chen to welcome Wei Jiu. After five attempts to persuade him, the King of Chen finally sent someone to establish Wei Jiu as King of Wei.

The King of Liang claimed illness and sent his general to lead troops to Handan. Gao Di was angry and sent someone to reproach the King of Liang. The King of Liang was afraid and wished to go personally to apologize. His general Hu Zhe said: 'Your Majesty did not go at first, but now that you see reproach and go, going will mean becoming a captive. It would be better to simply raise troops and rebel.' The Grand Historian says: Though Wei Bao and Peng Yue were originally of base status, they had already swept up a thousand li of territory, sat facing south and called themselves lords, their bloodied victories and triumphant advances were heard of daily. They harbored rebellious and treacherous intentions. When defeated, they did not die but were captured and imprisoned, their bodies subjected to execution. Why was this? Even those of middling talent and above would be ashamed of such conduct. How much more so for true kings! There was no other reason. Their wisdom and strategy surpassed others; they only suffered from lacking the proper status. Once they obtained even a small measure of authority, they rose like clouds and dragons in transformation. They wished to find a place where their talents could be properly employed, which is why they endured imprisonment without declining.
