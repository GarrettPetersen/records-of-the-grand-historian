The Grand Historian says: The enlightened kings of high antiquity who promoted music did so not to amuse their hearts and indulge in pleasure, nor to satisfy their desires and give free rein to lust, but to establish good governance. All proper teachings begin with sound; when sound is correct, conduct becomes correct. Thus music serves to stir the blood vessels, circulate the vital energy, and harmonize and correct the heart. Thus the gong note moves the spleen and harmonizes sagehood; the shang note moves the lung and harmonizes righteousness; the jue note moves the liver and harmonizes benevolence; the zhi note moves the heart and harmonizes ritual; the yu note moves the kidney and harmonizes wisdom. Thus music internally assists in correcting the heart and externally distinguishes between noble and base; Above it serves the ancestral temples; below it transforms the common people. A zither is eight chi and one cun long; this is the correct measure. The thickest string is the gong note, placed in the center; it represents the ruler. The shang is stretched to the right side; the others follow in order of size without losing their sequence; thus the positions of ruler and minister are correct. Thus hearing the gong sound makes people warm, comfortable, and magnanimous; Hearing the shang sound makes people upright and fond of righteousness; Hearing the jiao tone makes people compassionate and loving toward others; Hearing the zhi tone makes people delight in goodness and enjoy giving; Hearing the yu tone makes people orderly and fond of ritual. Indeed, ritual enters from outside, while music emerges from within. Therefore, the gentleman cannot for a moment leave ritual; for a moment leaving ritual, then violent and insolent conduct exhausts what is external; Nor can he for a moment leave music; for a moment leaving music, then treacherous and deviant conduct exhausts what is internal. Therefore, musical tones are what the gentleman uses to nurture righteousness. Indeed, in ancient times, the Son of Heaven and the lords listened to bells and chimes without ever leaving the courtyard; ministers and grand officers listened to qin and se music without ever leaving their presence; this was to nurture proper conduct and prevent licentious dissipation. Indeed, licentious dissipation arises from lack of ritual, therefore the sage kings made people hear the sounds of ya and song in their ears, see the rites of dignified bearing in their eyes, perform respectful demeanor with their feet, and speak the way of benevolence and righteousness with their mouths. Therefore, the gentleman speaks all day long and deviant ways have no way to enter.
