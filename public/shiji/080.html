Le Yi was a worthy man who loved military affairs. The people of Zhao recommended him. When King Wuling of Zhao had the rebellion at Shaqiu, Le Yi left Zhao and went to Wei. He heard that King Zhao of Yan, because of the rebellion of Zi Zhi, was greatly defeated by Qi. King Zhao of Yan resented Qi and never forgot for even one day to avenge himself on Qi. Yan was a small, remote state with insufficient power to control others. Thus King Zhao humbled himself and treated scholars with respect, first honoring Guo Wei to attract talented people. Thus Le Yi served as envoy for King Zhao of Wei to Yan, and the King of Yan received him with the courtesy due a guest. Le Yi declined with modesty, then presented his pledge and became a minister. King Zhao of Yan appointed him deputy prime minister, and this continued for a long time.
