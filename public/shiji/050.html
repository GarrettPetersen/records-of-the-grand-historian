Sui had been king of Zhao for twenty-six years. During Emperor Jing's reign, he was convicted through Chao Cuo's machinations and had his Changshan commandery stripped away. When Wu and Chu rose in rebellion, King Zhao Sui joined their conspiracy and raised troops. His chancellor Jiande and interior minister Wang Han urged him not to proceed, but he refused to heed their advice. Sui had Jiande and Wang Han burned to death, dispatched troops to garrison his western frontier, and planned to wait for Wu to advance west together. He sent envoys north to the Xiongnu, formed an alliance with them, and launched attacks on Han. Han dispatched Marquis Quzhou Li Ji to strike against him. King Zhao Sui retreated and defended the city of Handan. The standoff lasted seven months. Wu and Chu suffered defeat at Liang and were unable to advance westward. The Xiongnu learned of this and halted their advance, refusing to invade Han borders. Luan Bu returned from crushing Qi and combined forces to divert water and flood the city of Zhao. The city of Zhao was breached. King Zhao took his own life. Handan surrendered. The line of King Zhao You came to an end.
