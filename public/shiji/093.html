King Xin of Han was the illegitimate grandson of the former King Xiang of Han. He was eight chi and five cun tall. When Xiang Liang established the Later King Huai of Chu, Yan, Qi, Zhao, and Wei all already had former kings. Only Han lacked a successor. Therefore, he established one of Han's noble sons, Lord Hengyang Cheng, as King of Han, hoping to pacify and stabilize Han's traditional territories. Xiang Liang was defeated and died at Dingtao. Cheng fled to King Huai. Pei Gong led troops to attack Yangcheng. He sent Zhang Liang, along with the Han Situ, to accept the surrender of Han's traditional territories. He obtained Xin and made him a Han general. Xin led his troops and followed Pei Gong through Wuguan Pass.

After Pei Gong was established as King of Han, Han Xin followed him into Hanzhong. Xiang Ji enfeoffed the various kings and they all went to their states. Han King Cheng, because he had not followed and had no merit, was not sent to his state. Instead, he was made a liehou. King Xin of Han, Zhou Ke, and others defended Xingyang. When Chu defeated them at Xingyang, Xin surrendered to Chu. Soon after, he escaped and returned to Han. In winter of the seventh year, the emperor personally went to attack, breaking Xin's army at Tongdi and executing his general Wang Xi. Xin fled to the Xiongnu. He and the people of Baituâ€”Manqiuchen, Wang Huang, and othersâ€”established Zhao Li, a descendant of Zhao, as king. Han ordered the chariot cavalry to attack and break the Xiongnu. The Xiongnu constantly fled in defeat. Han, riding their victory, pursued northward. The Protector General Zhongwei Chen Ping spoke to the emperor: 'The Hu have their full forces. In spring of the eleventh year, the former Han King Xin again entered and dwelt at Canhe with Hu cavalry, resisting Han. Han sent General Chai to attack him, and sent Xin a letter saying: 'Your Majesty is magnanimous and benevolent.
