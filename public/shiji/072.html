When King Wu of Qin died without sons, his younger brother was established as King Zhao. King Zhao's mother was formerly titled Lady Mi the Eighth. When King Zhao ascended the throne, Lady Mi the Eighth was honored as Empress Dowager Xuan. Empress Dowager Xuan was not King Wu's mother. King Wu's mother was honored as Empress Huiwen and passed away before King Wu. Empress Dowager Xuan had two younger brothers: her elder half-brother from a different father was called Marquis of Rang, surnamed Wei, named Ran; her younger full brother was Mi Rong, who served as Lord of Huayang. King Zhao's full younger brothers were titled Lord of Gaoling and Lord of Jingyang. Wei Ran was the most capable of them. He held office and managed affairs from the time of King Hui and King Wu onward. When King Wu died, his younger brothers contended for the throne. Only Wei Ran exerted his strength to establish King Zhao. When King Zhao ascended the throne, he appointed Ran as general to guard Xianyang. He executed the rebellion of Ji Jun, expelled King Wu's empress to Wei, and exterminated all of King Zhao's disloyal brothers. His might shook the state of Qin. Since King Zhao was young, Empress Dowager Xuan governed personally and employed Wei Ran to conduct political affairs.
