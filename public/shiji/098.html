The Marquis of Yangling, Fu Kuan, followed as a cavalry general of the five great men of Wei. He became a retainer and arose from Hengyang. He followed in attacking Anyang and Gangli, struck Zhao Fen's army at Kaifeng, and also struck Yang Xiong at Quyu and Yangwu. He beheaded twelve enemies and was granted the marquisate of Qing. He followed and arrived at Bashang. When Pei Gong was established as Han King, the Han King granted Fu Kuan the title Lord Gongde. He followed in entering Hanzhong and was promoted to Right Cavalry General. He followed in pacifying the Three Qins and was granted the fief of Diaoyin. He followed in attacking Xiang Ji, waited at Huai, and was granted the marquisate of Tongde Hou. He followed in attacking Xiang Guan, Zhou Lan, and Long Qu. The troops he led beheaded one cavalry general below Ao, and his fief was increased.

The Marquis of Kuaicheng, Zhou Bo, was a man of Pei and of the Zhou clan. He often served Gaozu as chariot companion and followed him as a retainer when he arose from Pei. He arrived at Bashang, went west into Shu and Han, returned and pacified the Three Qins, and received the fief of Chiyang. He cut off the eastern Yongdao, followed and emerged crossing Pingyin, encountered the Marquis of Huaiyin's troops at Xiangguo. The army was now advantageous, now disadvantageous, but he finally had no heart that departed from the Sovereign. Therefore, 'Zhou was made Marquis Wu and given a fief of 3,300 households.' In Gaozu's twelfth year, Zhou was made Marquis of Kuaicheng, with previous fiefs eliminated.

The Grand Historian says: The Marquis of Yangling Fu Kuan and the Marquis of Xinwu Jin Xi both held high marquisates. They followed Gaozu arising from Shandong, attacked Xiang Ji, executed famous generals, broke armies and induced the surrender of cities numbering in the tens, and never experienced distress or humiliation. This too was heaven's bestowal. The Marquis of Kuaicheng Zhou Bo grasped a heart that was firm and upright, his person was never suspected. When the Sovereign desired to go somewhere, he never failed to shed tears. This was the appearance of one who had a wounded heart. He can be called a solid and thick gentleman.
