Lord Pingyuan, Zhao Sheng, was one of the noble scions of Zhao. Among the noble scions, Sheng was the most worthy. Lord Pingyuan's house tower overlooked a commoner's home. In the commoner's home lived a crippled man who, with his knees spread apart, crawled to draw water. Lord Pingyuan's concubine was dwelling in the tower above, looked down and saw him, and burst out laughing. The next day, the crippled man came to Lord Pingyuan's gate and made this request: 'I have heard that Your Lordship delights in worthy men. Worthy men travel thousands of li without hesitation to reach you, because you honor the worthy and humble the unworthy. Alas, I suffer from a debilitating illness. Yet one of Your Lordship's concubines looked down upon me and laughed. I wish to have the head of the one who laughed at me.' Lord Pingyuan laughed and replied, 'Very well.' The crippled man departed, and Lord Pingyuan laughed, saying: 'Look at this wretched fellow! He wants to kill my beautiful concubine just because of a single laugh. Isn't this going too far!' In the end, he did not kill her. More than a year passed, and gradually more than half of the retainers in his household had departed. Lord Pingyuan was puzzled and said: 'I, Sheng, have never dared to treat you gentlemen discourteously. Why have so many departed?' One of his retainers stepped forward and replied: 'Because Your Lordship did not kill the concubine who laughed at the cripple, they think you value beauty over worth. The worthy men have departed.' Thereupon Lord Pingyuan had the concubine who laughed at the cripple beheaded. He personally went to the gate and invited the cripple in, then apologized to him. Afterward, his retainers gradually began to return once more. At this time, Qi had Lord Mengchang, Wei had Lord Xinling, and Chu had Lord Chunshen. Thus they vied with one another to attract worthy men with generous treatment.

When Qin besieged Handan, Zhao sent Lord Pingyuan to seek aid. He allied with Chu and brought along twenty of his retainers who possessed both martial valor and literary accomplishment. Lord Pingyuan said: 'If diplomacy can secure victory, that would be excellent. If diplomacy cannot prevail, then we shall perform the blood oath beneath the ornate hall and secure a firm alliance before returning.' The nineteen others exchanged glances and smiled at him, but did not reject him.
