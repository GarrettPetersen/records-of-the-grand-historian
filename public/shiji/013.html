Master Chu said: 'Not so. The Poetry speaks of Jie being born from an egg and Hou Ji from human footprints, only wishing to show they had heaven's mandate and sincere intentions. Ghosts and spirits cannot complete themselves, they must be born through people. How could they be born without fathers! One says they had fathers, one says they had no fathers. Transmit what is believable as believable, transmit doubts as doubts, therefore both are spoken. Yao knew Jie and Ji were both worthy people, born by heaven, therefore enfeoffed Jie with seventy li, and after ten-plus generations came to Tang who became king of tianxia. Yao knew Hou Ji's descendants would later be kings, therefore additionally enfeoffed him with a hundred li. His later generations lasted nearly a thousand years, until Wen Wang then had tianxia. The Poetry transmission said: 'Tang's ancestor was Jie, born without a father. Jie's mother was bathing with her sisters at Xuanqiu water when a swallow holding an egg dropped it. Jie's mother got it, therefore held it, mistakenly swallowed it, and immediately gave birth to Jie. Jie was born worthy. Yao established him as Situ and surnamed him the Zi clan. Zi means Ci; Ci means increasingly great. The poets praised and sang of him saying 'The Yin altar is vast and vast, heaven's mandate dark bird descended and gave birth to Shang'. Shang is solid, Yin's designation. Wen Wang's ancestor was Hou Ji, Hou Ji also born without a father. Hou Ji's mother was Jiang Yuan. She went out and saw great person footprints, stepped on them, knew it in her body, then gave birth to Hou Ji. Jiang Yuan took it as having no father, lowly abandoned it in the middle of the road, but cattle and sheep avoided and did not trample it. She embraced it in the mountains, and mountain people nurtured it. Again she abandoned it in the great marsh, but birds covered and warmed and fed it. Jiang Yuan wondered at it. Thereupon she knew it was a Son of Heaven, then took and raised it. Yao knew his worthy talent, established him as great farmer, surnamed him the Ji clan. Ji means root. The poets praised and sang of him saying 'its beginning gave birth to the people', deeply cultivated and increasingly accomplished, speaking of Hou Ji's beginning.' Confucius said: 'Former ones Yao commanded Jie as the Zi clan, because he would have Tang. Commanded Hou Ji as the Ji clan, because he would have Wen Wang. The Great King commanded Ji Li, made clear heaven's auspicious signs. Tai Bo went to Wu, then gave birth to the source.' Heaven's mandate is difficult to speak of, only sages can see it. Shun, Yu, Jie, and Hou Ji are all Yellow Emperor descendants. The Yellow Emperor tallied heaven's mandate and governed tianxia. His virtue and favor were deep for later generations, therefore his descendants were all again established as Sons of Heaven. This is heaven repaying the virtuous. People did not know, thinking he idly followed and arose from a plain-clothed commoner only. Indeed, how could a plain-clothed commoner arise and become king of tianxia without reason? It is because he had heaven's mandate.'

The Yellow Emperor is such a one. When the five governments are clear then repair ritual and righteousness, follow heaven's timing to raise troops and attack those who are beneficial - such ones become kings, and have fortune for a thousand generations. The Shu king is also of the Yellow Emperor's later generations. Until now he is five thousand li southwest of Han, often comes to court and submit, transports tribute to Han.
