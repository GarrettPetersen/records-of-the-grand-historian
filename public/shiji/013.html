He said: 'The tradition says that rulers of All Under Heaven who for ten thousand people redeem the people's fate are emperors, and have blessing for ten thousand generations. The Yellow Emperor was such. When the five government affairs are clear, then cultivate ritual and righteousness, follow Heaven's seasons to raise troops and campaign successfullyâ€”that is ruling, with blessing for a thousand generations. The King of Shu was Yellow Emperor descendants; to this day in the southwest five thousand li from Han, they constantly come to court and pay tribute to Hanâ€”not because their ancestors had virtue that flowed to later generations? How can practicing morality be neglected? Ruling lords should raise this up and observe it. The Han General Huo Qubing, named Guang, was also Yellow Emperor descendants. This can be told to those of broad learning and far sight; it is difficult to explain to those of shallow knowledge. Why speak thus? Ancient feudal lords took countries as surnames. Huo was a country name. King Wu enfeoffed his younger brother Shu at Huo; later generations Duke Xian of Jin extinguished Duke Huo, later generations became commoners, coming and going dwelling at Pingyang. Pingyang is in Hedong, Hedong is Jin territory, divided into Wei country. Speaking with the Poetry, it can also be considered Zhou generations. Zhou arose from Hou Ji; Hou Ji was born without a father. Speaking with the Three Dynasties genealogical transmissions, Hou Ji had a father named Gao Xin; Gao Xin was Yellow Emperor's great-grandson. The Yellow Emperor beginning and end transmission says: 'When Han rose, more than a century, there will be a person not short not tall, coming from Yan's white region, holding All Under Heaven's government; at that time there will be an infant ruler, desiring to proceed by cart.' General Huo originally dwelt at Pingyang in Yan. When I was a gentleman attendant, with the fangshi I examined merit at the banner pavilion below, and he told me. Is this not magnificent!'
