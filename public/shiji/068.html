Lord Shang was a bastard son of a branch of the Wei nobility, named Yang, surnamed Gongsun, his ancestry originally of the Ji surname. Yang in youth delighted in the study of law and names, served as junior aide to Wei chancellor Gongshu Zuo. Gongshu Zuo knew his worth, but had not yet advanced him. Just then Zuo fell ill, King Hui of Wei personally went to inquire after his illness, said: 'If chancellor should die, what shall we do for the state?' Gongshu said: 'My junior aide Gongsun Yang, though young, has extraordinary talent, I wish Your Majesty would entrust the entire state to him.' The king was silent. The king was about to leave when Zuo dismissed his attendants and said: 'If Your Majesty does not employ Yang, you must kill him, do not let him leave the borders.' The king agreed and departed. Gongshu Zuo summoned Yang and apologized: 'Just now when the king asked who could be chancellor, I recommended you, but the king's expression did not approve. I am putting the ruler first and ministers second, so I told the king that if he does not employ Yang, he should kill him. The king agreed to me. You should leave quickly, you will soon be captured.' Yang said: 'If he cannot employ you by following your recommendation to use me, how can he employ you by following your recommendation to kill me?' He finally did not leave. King Hui having departed, he told his attendants: 'Gongshu is gravely ill, how lamentable, he wants me to entrust the state to Gongsun Yang, is this not absurd!'

Gongshu having died, Gongsun Yang heard that Duke Xiao of Qin had issued an edict throughout the state seeking worthy men, intending to revive Duke Mu's achievements and eastward recover lost territories, so he went west to Qin, through Duke Xiao's favored minister Jing Jian to seek audience with Duke Xiao. Duke Xiao having seen Wei Yang, spoke of affairs for a long time, Duke Xiao frequently dozed off, did not listen. Retiring, Duke Xiao angrily rebuked Jing Jian: 'Your guest is a charlatan, how can he be employed!' Jing Jian relayed this to Wei Yang. Duke Xiao having employed Wei Yang, Yang wished to change the laws, feared the world would criticize him. Wei Yang said: 'Doubtful actions have no reputation, doubtful affairs have no merit. Moreover those who practice superior conduct are surely criticized by the age; Those with unique wisdom are surely scorned by the people. Fools are dark about completed affairs, wise men see things at their inception. The people cannot be consulted about beginnings but can enjoy successes. Those who discuss supreme virtue do not harmonize with custom, those who accomplish great merit do not consult the multitude. Therefore sages if they can strengthen the state, do not follow old laws; If they can benefit the people, do not follow old rituals.' Duke Xiao said: "Good." Gan Long said: "Not so. Sages do not change the people and teach, wise men do not alter laws and govern. Following the people and teaching, without labor succeeds; Following laws and governing, officials are familiar and people at peace with them." Wei Yang said: "What Long says is the talk of common customs. Ordinary people are at peace with old customs, scholars drown in what they have heard. With these two occupying office and keeping laws is acceptable, but not for discussing beyond the laws. The Three Dynasties differed in ritual yet became kings, the Five Hegemons differed in law yet became hegemons. Wise men create laws, fools are restricted by them; Worthy men change rituals, worthless men are bound by them." Du Zhi said: "Benefit not a hundredfold, do not change law; Merit not tenfold, do not change implements. Following antiquity has no fault, following ritual has no error." Wei Yang said: "Governing the age is not one way, benefiting the state does not follow antiquity. Therefore Tang and Wu did not follow antiquity and became kings, Xia and Yin did not change ritual and perished. Employed Wei Yang as Left Senior Chamberlain, finally established the change law edict.
