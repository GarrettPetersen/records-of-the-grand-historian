King Wei Man of Joseon was originally a man of Yan. From the beginning when Yan was intact, they often raided and controlled Zhendan and Joseon, establishing officials and building barrier forts. Qin destroyed Yan, and it became part of the outer defenses of Liaodong. When Han arose, because it was distant and difficult to defend, they repaired the old Liaodong border fortifications, using the Hun River as the boundary, and assigned it to Yan. When Yan king Lu Wan rebelled and fled to the Xiongnu, Wei Man fled, gathered over a thousand followers, braided their hair and wore barbarian clothing, and went east out of the passes, crossed the Hun River, and settled in the old Qin empty lands between the upper and lower barriers, gradually subjugating Zhendan, Joseon barbarians, and former Yan and Qi fugitives, ruling them from Wanggeom Fortress.

During the time of Emperor Hui and Empress Gao, when the world was first pacified, the Liaodong governor made an agreement with Wei Man to serve as an outer subject, protecting the barbarians outside the border and preventing them from raiding the frontier; Any barbarian chiefs who wished to enter and see the Son of Heaven should not be prohibited. This was reported to the emperor, who approved it, so Wei Man used military might and wealth to invade and subjugate neighboring small settlements, and Zhendan and Lintun all came to submit and belong to him, covering several thousand square li.

He passed it to his son and grandson Right Channel, who lured ever more Han fugitives, and never once entered to pay respects; The neighboring countries like Zhendan wished to submit memorials to see the Son of Heaven, but he blocked and obstructed them from communicating. In the second year of Yuanshou, Han sent She He to reproach Right Channel, but he ultimately refused to obey the imperial edict. He left and reached the border at the Hun River, had his chariot driver stab and kill the Joseon vice-king Chang who was escorting him, then immediately crossed and galloped into the passes, returning to report to the Son of Heaven saying 'killed the Joseon general'. The emperor, for the beauty of his name, did not investigate, and appointed He as the Dongbu prefect of Liaodong. Joseon resented He, sent troops to attack and kill him.

The Son of Heaven recruited criminals to attack Joseon. That autumn, he sent the Tower Ship General Yang Pu from Qi by sea across the Bohai; With 50,000 troops, and the Left General Xun Zhi from Liaodong to attack Right Channel. Right Channel sent troops to defend the dangerous passes. The Left General's army officers, led by Du Zheng, first charged with the Liaodong troops, were defeated and scattered, many retreated and fled, so Du Zheng was executed by law. The Tower Ship General led 7,000 Qi troops and arrived first at Wanggeom Fortress. Right Channel defended the city, saw that the tower ship army was small, immediately came out of the city to attack the tower ships, the tower ship army was defeated and scattered in flight. General Yang Pu lost his troops, fled into the mountains for over ten days, gradually gathered the scattered soldiers, and reformed them. The Left General attacked the Joseon army west of the Hun River, could not break through from the front.

He sent his crown prince to apologize, offering five thousand horses, and supplying army provisions. With a crowd of over ten thousand people bearing arms, when they were about to cross the Hun River, the envoy and Left General suspected they would rebel, told the crown prince that since he had already submitted, the people should not bear arms. The crown prince also suspected the envoy and Left General would deceitfully kill him, so did not cross the Hun River, and led them back again. Shan returned and reported to the Son of Heaven, who executed Shan.

The Left General had formerly been a palace attendant, was favored, commanded Yan and Dai troops, who were fierce, rode on victory, the army was mostly arrogant. The tower ship general commanded Qi troops who entered by sea, had already suffered many defeats and losses; Previously when they fought Right Channel, they suffered and lost troops, the troops were all fearful, the general's heart was ashamed, when surrounding Right Channel, they often held peace tallies. The Left General urgently attacked, the Joseon ministers secretly sent people to privately agree to surrender to the tower ships, coming and going with messages, but not yet willing to decide. The Left General repeatedly made battle appointments with the tower ships, the tower ships wished to quickly fulfill their agreement, did not meet; The Left General also sent people to seek to secretly persuade Joseon to surrender, Joseon refused, their hearts attached to the tower ships: therefore the two generals could not cooperate. The Left General suspected the tower ships previously had the crime of losing troops, now secretly befriended Joseon and still would not surrender, suspected they had rebellious plans, did not dare to act. The Son of Heaven said the generals could not advance, previously sent Wei Shan to persuade Right Channel to surrender, Right Channel sent his crown prince, Shan could not decisively handle it, clashed with the Left General's plans, ultimately ruined the agreement. Now the two generals surround the city, but are at odds, therefore long undecided. Sent the Jinan governor Gongsun Sui to correct it, with discretion to handle affairs. Sui arrived, the Left General said: 'Joseon should have surrendered long ago, not surrendering has a reason.' He told Sui about the tower ships repeatedly not meeting appointments, fully informed him of his longstanding suspicions, saying: 'Now like this not taking it, I fear it will become a great harm, not only the tower ships, but also together with Joseon will destroy our army.' Sui also thought it so, and used the tally to summon the tower ship general into the Left General's camp to discuss matters, immediately ordered the Left General's subordinates to arrest the tower ship general and his army, reporting to the Son of Heaven. The Son of Heaven executed Sui.

Yin, Xi, Luo Ren all fled and surrendered to Han. Luo Ren died en route. In the third year of Yuanshou, summer, Ni Xi chancellor Shen sent people to kill Joseon king Right Channel and come surrender. Wanggeom Fortress was not yet taken, therefore Right Channel's minister Cheng Ji again rebelled, attacked the officials again. The Left General made Right Channel's son Chang surrender, and Luo Ren's son Zui announce and persuade their people, executed Cheng Ji, therefore finally pacified Joseon, making it four commanderies.
